CHAPTER 03 / PHOTIC PENETRATION

Light Layers

Sunlight does not descend uniformly. Each wavelength is filtered, absorbed, and scattered at a different rate. By 200 meters, the visible spectrum has collapsed to a faint, monochrome blue — and beyond, only darkness, broken solely by bioluminescent code.

§ 3.1 — SPECTRAL ATTENUATION

How wavelengths die in water

Water acts as a selective filter. Long-wavelength reds are absorbed within meters of the surface; cooler, shorter wavelengths persist deeper, until only a narrow band of blue-green photons reach the threshold of the dysphotic zone at 200 meters.

§ 3.3 — PHOTON BUDGET

Key thresholds

1%
Surface light at 100m
The 1% threshold conventionally defines the lower bound of the photic (euphotic) zone where photosynthesis remains viable.
200m
Photic / Dysphotic boundary
Below this depth, sunlight is insufficient to drive photosynthesis. Only blue-green photons penetrate further.
1000m
Dysphotic / Aphotic boundary
Below 1 km, no measurable sunlight remains. The only light is biological — bioluminescent flashes from resident fauna.
90%
Of the ocean is aphotic
By volume, nearly the entire ocean exists in permanent darkness. The sunlit surface is a thin, exceptional film.
