Five Strata of Crushing Dark.

Below the sunlit film of the surface, the ocean stacks itself into five pelagic zones — each one denser, darker, and more silently violent than the last. Pressure climbs by roughly one atmosphere for every ten meters descended; at the Challenger Deep, every square centimeter of hull bears the weight of a small car.
