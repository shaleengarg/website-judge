Into the Deep

What Survives the Trip

Modern hadal-rated submersibles share three traits: a near-perfect titanium sphere (the only geometry that distributes pressure equally), variable ballast measured in steel shot rather than tanks, and life-support sized for emergencies that may not have surface contact. The cabin volume rarely exceeds 1.5 m³ — about the size of a chest freezer — for trips that can exceed eleven hours.
