1%
Surface light at 100m
The 1% threshold conventionally defines the lower bound of the photic (euphotic) zone where photosynthesis remains viable.
200m
Photic / Dysphotic boundary
Below this depth, sunlight is insufficient to drive photosynthesis. Only blue-green photons penetrate further.
1000m
Dysphotic / Aphotic boundary
Below 1 km, no measurable sunlight remains. The only light is biological — bioluminescent flashes from resident fauna.
90%
Of the ocean is aphotic
By volume, nearly the entire ocean exists in permanent darkness. The sunlit surface is a thin, exceptional film.
