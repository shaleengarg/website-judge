The Poet

Solenne Vray, b. Mulhouse, 1981.

Solenne Vray spent eleven years as an industrial archivist for the Conservatoire du patrimoine sidérurgique in Metz before publishing a single line of verse. Her job, as she has described it, was to catalogue what could not be kept: shift rosters, freight ledgers, the carbon copies of letters dictated by foremen who had been dead for decades. She read these documents the way other poets read Rilke. They taught her, she says, that grief is mostly clerical.

Her first poems were written in the margins of those archives — never on the documents themselves, which she handled with cotton gloves, but on the back of timecard envelopes she carried home in her satchel. She wrote slowly, often only a stanza a month, and showed nothing to anyone until 2019, when a single sequence appeared in Revue Nu under the half-name S. V.

In the winter of 2021 she was awarded a six-month residency at the Abbaye de Fontevraud, where she completed the manuscript that would become The Hour That Eats Itself. The seventeen poems that emerged are set, almost without exception, in the mill towns of the Moselle and the Meuse — Hayange, Thionville, Algrange — places her grandmother left in 1962 and never spoke of again.

I write toward the objects my grandmother could not name. — Interview, Revue Nu, 2023

Préoccupations

On the word déprise.

Vray returns, in interviews and in the poems themselves, to the French noun déprise — a word for which English has no clean equivalent. It names the slackening of a grip, the moment a held thing is no longer held, the gradual loosening of a faculty or an attention. It is the word a doctor uses for cognitive decline and the word a climber uses for the second before a fall. It is also, she has noted, what a foundry does to the iron at the end of its shift.

Much of The Hour That Eats Itself is an attempt to translate this single word across seventeen different rooms. The translation never settles. That is, she insists, the point.

A Brief Chronology

Selected dates.

1981
Born in Mulhouse, Haut-Rhin. Raised in Audun-le-Tiche.
2004
Licence in history, Université de Lorraine, Nancy.
2008–2019
Archivist, Conservatoire du patrimoine sidérurgique, Metz.
2019
First sequence published in Revue Nu, signed S. V.
2021–2022
Residency, Abbaye de Fontevraud, Maine-et-Loire.
2024
The Hour That Eats Itself, debut collection.

She lives in a third-floor apartment in Nancy, beside a rail line that no longer carries ore. She has said she will not write a second book until she has finished forgetting the first.
