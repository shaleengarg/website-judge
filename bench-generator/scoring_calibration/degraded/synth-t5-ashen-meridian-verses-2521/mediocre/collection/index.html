The Hour That Eats Itself

Collection

Seventeen poems on industrial grief, inherited silence, and the geography of the eastern French mill towns. Composed between 2017 and 2023 in Mulhouse, Thionville, and the Abbaye de Fontevraud.

Index of Poems

Seventeen, in order.

    Foundry, 1973

    — the iron, at last, gave a softer name to morning.
  2. II.

    — a long grey sentence the river refused to finish.

    — a word the language keeps in a locked drawer.

    Annunciation at the Rolling Mill

    — and the angel, soot-mouthed, asked nothing of me.
  8. VIII.

    Inventory of a Father's Toolbox

    — each handle worn to the shape of someone else's grip.
  9. IX.

    Thionville, Tuesday

    — the bells corrected the hour the factory could not keep.
  10. X.

    Hour That Eats Itself

    — noon arrived already devoured at the edges.
  11. XI.

    The Archivist's Refusal

    — I filed the silence under its proper year.
  12. XII.

    Slow Train, Metz–Longwy

    — the windows kept giving back fields we had already crossed.
  13. XIII.

    Slate Roofs After Rain

    — even the houses turned their faces toward the dark.
  14. XIV.

    A Photograph My Grandmother Cropped

  15. XV.

    Coke Oven, Sunday

  16. XVI.

    Wage, Translated

    — what was paid for, and what was simply taken.
  17. XVII.

    Threshold, Without Door

    — I stood there long enough to become the frame.
