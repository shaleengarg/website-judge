I.

Foundry, 1973

after a photograph of my grandfather, third shift

The light here is not light.
It is the memory of light
held inside iron a half-second longer
than the body that poured it.
My grandfather’s hands —
I never saw them clean.
Even washed, they kept the metal
in the half-moons of the nails,
a residue more durable than name.

Outside, the foundry continued
to be a foundry. The hour
ate the hour before it,
and the men, walking the road
in their grey coveralls,
looked like nothing so much
as the photograph
a photograph would later become.
