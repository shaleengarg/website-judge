For the Gremolata

  • 1 small bunchflat-leaf parsley, finely chopped

  • 1preserved lemon rind, finely diced
  • 1 tbspgood olive oil

A note on preserved lemons. Seek out the small, soft Beldi-style lemons cured in their own juice and salt — the kind sold loose at North African grocers in a cloudy yellow brine. Supermarket jars in clear, vinegared liquid will work in a pinch, but the flavour reads thinner and a touch metallic against the lamb. Rinse before using, and don't discard the rind: that's where the perfume lives.
