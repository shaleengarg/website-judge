In her late twenties she spent two years living in Marrakech, working part-time at a small riad and falling in love with the medina's spice merchants, the brined lemons that turn up in everything, and the slow rhythm of tagine cookery. The lamb shoulder on this site is the dish she has cooked more than any other since coming home — a quiet collision of her grandmother's braising instincts and the preserved-lemon pantry she carried back from Morocco. She is not a chef, not a food writer, just a home cook who wanted one recipe written down properly.
