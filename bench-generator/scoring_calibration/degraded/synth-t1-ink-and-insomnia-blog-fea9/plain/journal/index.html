Recent entries, written between midnight and the first métro.

A 2 a.m. walk on Rue Saint-Denis

November 14, 2024·walks

The bars had closed an hour earlier and the street had that swept-clean quality it only gets in November, when the wind moves cigarette ash but no leaves. I walked from Sherbrooke down to Mont-Royal without seeing more than four people, and three of them were waiting for the same bus.

There was a man in a doorway reading a paperback by phone light. I didn't see the title. I wanted to ask but kept walking, the way you do when you understand someone has chosen their solitude on purpose.

Power outage, twenty-two minutes

November 7, 2024·apartment

The lights went at 11:48 and came back at 12:10, and in between the building made sounds I had never heard it make — a settling, almost a sigh. I lit two candles I had been saving for nothing in particular and sat on the floor by the window.

The street below stayed dark longer than the apartments. From four storeys up you could see exactly which neighbours had been awake: a square of kitchen, a blue television, a woman in a green sweater leaning into a refrigerator that no longer hummed.

The stray on the fire escape

October 29, 2024·company

He has been coming for three nights now — a thin tabby with one torn ear and an air of having read more books than I have. I left out a saucer of water and pretended not to watch from behind the curtain.

He drank, sat for a long time looking at the alley, and then turned and looked directly at the window where I was hiding. I don't know what he saw. I don't know what I was hoping he'd see.

Translating a sentence for an hour

October 21, 2024·work

I spent most of the night with one sentence from a French essay about provincial libraries — a sentence whose verb sat in the middle like a stone in a stream, parting everything around it. In English the verb wants to be at the front, eager, undignified.

By 3 a.m. I had four versions and liked none of them. I went to bed and dreamed in subjunctives. In the morning the first version was the right one. It usually is.

The all-night dépanneur

October 12, 2024·neighbourhood

There is a dépanneur on the corner that has been open every hour I have ever needed it to be, run by a man who reads thick history books behind the counter and never looks up until you set something down. Last night it was a tin of sardines and a clementine.

He weighed the clementine in his hand, said celle-ci est bonne, and went back to his book. It felt like a small benediction. I ate the clementine walking home and saved the sardines for a less hopeful evening.
