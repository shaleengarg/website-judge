About Mara Solène

Montreal · writing since 2019

I spent the better part of a decade as a reference librarian in the Plateau, shelving novels and answering questions about microfilm, before drifting into freelance copywriting. These days I write quiet, careful sentences for small magazines and the occasional brand that promises not to ask for exclamation points. Montreal is my permanent address, in winter and otherwise.

Ink & Insomnia began in February 2019, during a stretch of sleepless weeks when nothing else seemed to fit the shape of the night. I started keeping a notebook by the kettle and posting fragments online the next morning — observations on the street outside, the books open on my floor, the texture of being awake while the city wasn't. The blog has carried on, more or less, ever since.

Outside of writing, I translate now and then between French and English, mostly poetry that nobody asked me to translate. I shoot film photography on an old Pentax, develop the rolls slowly, and travel by train whenever I can justify it. I'm interested in libraries, footnotes, weather, and long walks that end in unfamiliar neighborhoods.

If you'd like to write to me, please visit the contact page.
