What I've Been Reading

A running shelf — updated when the insomnia obliges

Currently

  • — Annie Ernaux, The Years A book that reads like a photograph album rewritten in the first-person plural.
  • — W.G. Sebald, Austerlitz Re-reading slowly, one long paragraph at a time, mostly after midnight.
  • — Nicole Brossard, Mauve Desert
  • — Marguerite Duras, Writing Brief, mineral, and somehow louder each time I open it.

Finished This Year

  • — Rachel Cusk, Outline
  • — Jenny Offill, Department of Speculation Read in a single sitting on the metro between Berri-UQAM and Jean-Talon.
  • — Mavis Gallant, Paris Stories
  • — Olga Tokarczuk, Flights
  • — Heather Christle, The Crying Book Tender, fragmentary, the kind of essay that knows when to stop.
  • — Roland Barthes, A Lover's Discourse
  • — Dany Laferrière, L'énigme du retour

Abandoned

  • — Karl Ove Knausgård, My Struggle, Vol. 4 Loved the first three; ran out of patience somewhere in northern Norway.
  • — Hanya Yanagihara, To Paradise
  • — Thomas Bernhard, Correction Not abandoned, exactly — postponed until a colder, quieter winter.
  • — Ottessa Moshfegh, Lapvona
