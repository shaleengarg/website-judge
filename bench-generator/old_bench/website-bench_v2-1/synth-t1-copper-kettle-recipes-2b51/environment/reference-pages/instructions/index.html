Hungarian Beef Goulash
Step-by-Step

How to Make It

  1. Sear the beef. Heat 2 tablespoons of lard in a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high heat and brown the cubed chuck in batches, about 3 minutes per side, until deeply caramelized. Transfer the meat to a plate and reserve the rendered fat.
  2. Sweat the onions. Lower the heat to medium and add the sliced yellow onions to the pot, stirring occasionally for 10–12 minutes until soft, golden, and jammy. Add the minced garlic in the last minute so it perfumes the fat without burning.
  3. Bloom the paprika. Pull the pot off the heat entirely, then stir in 3 tablespoons of sweet Hungarian paprika and a pinch of caraway, coating the onions thoroughly. Working off heat prevents the paprika from scorching, which would turn the whole pot bitter.
  4. Deglaze with beef stock. Return the pot to medium heat and pour in 1 cup of warm stock, scraping up every browned bit from the bottom with a wooden spoon. Add the seared beef back along with its juices, the diced green pepper, and a single bay leaf.
  5. Simmer low and slow. Add enough remaining stock to just cover the meat, bring to a bare tremble — never a rolling boil — then cover and braise for 2 hours, stirring every 30 minutes. The goulash is ready when the beef yields to a spoon and the sauce runs glossy and brick-red.
  6. Finish and rest. Stir in the diced potatoes during the final 30 minutes of cooking and season generously with salt to taste. Let the pot rest off the heat for 10 minutes before ladling into warm bowls.
