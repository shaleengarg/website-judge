The Pressure on Blue Systems

Four converging forces are restructuring the chemistry, temperature and architecture of Earth's oceans. The data is unambiguous; the pace is accelerating.

Chapter 05 Sources: IPCC AR6, NOAA, IUCN Updated 2024
// Threat Matrix
Four vectors reshaping the ocean
01 / CRYOSPHERE
Melting Ice Caps
Greenland and Antarctic ice sheets shed 270 Gt annually. Freshwater influx weakens deep-water formation and stalls the global conveyor.
02 / CHEMISTRY
Acidification
Oceans absorb ~30% of anthropogenic CO₂, dissolving shells of pteropods, corals and foraminifera at industrial-era rates.
03 / EXTRACTION
Overfishing
Industrial trawling sweeps 5× the area of all logged forests yearly. One-third of monitored fisheries now operate below biological limits.
04 / POLLUTION
Plastic Influx
An estimated 11 million tonnes of plastic flow into the ocean yearly, fragmenting into microplastics now found in every depth zone.
