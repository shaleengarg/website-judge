The Ocean in Five Strata

From sunlit shallows to the crushing trenches, Earth's oceans organize themselves into five distinct biotic and physical strata. Each zone is defined by light, pressure, temperature — and the lifeforms that adapted to survive there.

Sunlight

0 – 200 m

Epipelagic. Photosynthesis powers nearly all marine food webs from this thin surface layer.

Twilight

200 – 1,000 m

Mesopelagic. Faint blue light remains. Home to bioluminescent fish and daily vertical migrations.

Midnight

1,000 – 4,000 m

Bathypelagic. Zero sunlight. Cold (4°C), still water dominated by predator-prey ambush adaptations.

Abyssal

4,000 – 6,000 m

Abyssopelagic. Vast plains of marine snow. Pressure exceeds 400 atmospheres at depth.

Hadal

6,000 – 11,000 m

The trenches. Named for Hades. Endemic amphipods thrive in absolute darkness and bone-crushing pressure.
