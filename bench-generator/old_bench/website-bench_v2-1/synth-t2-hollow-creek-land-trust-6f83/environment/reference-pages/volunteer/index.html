Volunteer Stewardship

From spring trail openings to autumn seed collection, our volunteer stewards are the hands and feet of Hollow Creek's conservation mission. Each season brings new opportunities to walk the ridges, wade the creeks, and protect the forested watersheds we hold in trust — no prior experience required, just curiosity, sturdy boots, and a willingness to learn from the land.

Current Volunteer Roles

Trail Crew

Help maintain over 38 miles of public access trails across our easement properties. Work includes brushing tread, installing water bars, and repairing footbridges alongside our stewardship coordinator.

Commitment · 2 Saturdays / month · Mar–Nov

Invasive Species Removal

Join field crews removing autumn olive, multiflora rose, and Japanese stiltgrass from sensitive riparian zones. Training in plant ID and safe hand-tool use is provided onsite each session.

Commitment · 4 hrs / month · Year-round

Water Quality Sampling

Collect monthly stream samples from twelve fixed monitoring stations across the Hollow Creek watershed. Data feeds directly into our published annual creek-health report and partner research efforts.

Commitment · 1 morning / month · Year-round

Boundary Monitoring

Walk easement boundaries with a trained partner to document conditions, photograph corners, and note any encroachments or changes. Most monitoring routes are completed once per property each year.

Commitment · 1–2 days / year per property

School Program Educator

Lead hands-on field lessons for visiting K–8 classes at our Tuckasegee Bluffs outdoor classroom. Curriculum and lesson plans are provided; we ask for a background check and one orientation session.

Commitment · 3–6 field days / school year

Event Support

Assist with our annual Creek Day festival, donor hikes, and quarterly volunteer potlucks. Roles range from registration desk and parking to potluck setup — perfect for first-time volunteers.

Commitment · Flexible · 4–6 events / year

Fill out a short interest form and our Stewardship Coordinator, Deja Whitfield, will be in touch within two weeks to match you with a role that fits your skills and schedule.

Our Mission

Hollow Creek Land Trust protects forested ridgeline and creek-bottom habitat across the southern Appalachians through conservation easements, community partnerships, and volunteer stewardship — keeping working lands working and wild lands wild.
