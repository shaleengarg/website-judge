Our Land

From creek-bottom hardwood coves to wind-carved ridgelines, the lands we hold in trust span four counties of the southern Appalachian highlands — a living mosaic of forest, water, and working family farms.

Featured Properties

A closer look at three of the keystone tracts under permanent conservation easement — each chosen for its ecological value, watershed function, and the families who have stewarded it for generations.

FLOODPLAIN FOREST
Sycamore Bottoms
840 acres · Clay County

A wide creek-bottom hardwood forest along the lower Hollow Creek, home to nesting wood ducks, mature sycamores, and three miles of clear, gravel-bedded stream.

Riparian
RIDGELINE WOODLAND
Ridgeback Hollow
1,200 acres · Macon County

Steep oak-hickory slopes climbing to a quartzite ridge crest. Critical migratory songbird habitat with an unbroken canopy stretching across two adjacent easements.

Upland Forest
RIVER BLUFF
Tuckasegee Bluffs
620 acres · Jackson County

Dramatic limestone bluffs overlooking the Tuckasegee River, harboring rare calciphile wildflowers and a known hibernaculum for the northern long-eared bat.

Cliff & River
