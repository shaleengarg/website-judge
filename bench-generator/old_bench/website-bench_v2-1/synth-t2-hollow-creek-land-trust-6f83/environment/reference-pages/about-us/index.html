Our Story

Hollow Creek Land Trust was founded in 2003 by a coalition of Clay County farmers, foresters, and environmental educators who watched the ridgelines around them parcel out, year by year, into vacation tracts and timber speculation. They believed a working Appalachian landscape — one where families could still farm, hunt, gather ramps, and walk the creeks — was worth protecting through the patient, unglamorous work of conservation easements.

Two decades later, we hold 47 easements across five counties, totaling more than 12,400 acres of forested ridgeline and creek-bottom habitat. Every parcel was protected by a willing landowner, sometimes after years of conversation around kitchen tables.

"We don't buy the land. We build the trust that keeps it."

Today our small team works alongside dozens of volunteer stewards, partner agencies, university researchers, and the descendants of our founding families. The work is local, slow, and rooted — much like the white oaks we walk beneath.
