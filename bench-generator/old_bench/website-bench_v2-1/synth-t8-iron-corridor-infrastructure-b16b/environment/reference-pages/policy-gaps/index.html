§ 4.2 — PROMISE vs. PERFORMANCE

FAST Act, Slow Money

2015 AUTHORIZATION · 2023 GAO AUDIT FINDINGS
2015 · WHAT WAS PROMISED

A decade of structural renewal.

The Fixing America's Surface Transportation Act allocated unprecedented multi-year funding for short-line and regional rail, with explicit set-asides for bridge replacement, grade-crossing modernization, and Positive Train Control build-out across Class II carriers.

Congressional sponsors described the program as the largest single commitment to inland freight infrastructure since the Staggers Act of 1980.

2023 · WHAT WAS DELIVERED

A ledger of deferred obligation.

GAO auditors found that less than 25% of authorized funds had reached disbursement by the program's eighth year. Of the bridges flagged for replacement in the original FAST docket, only 412 had completed structural work; 879 remained in queue without a scheduled start date.

Three of five eligible Class II carriers withdrew applications mid-cycle, citing administrative complexity.

§ 4.3 — LEGISLATIVE PATHWAY

Eight Recommendations

DRAWN FROM 62 FIELD INTERVIEWS & FEDERAL DOCKET REVIEW
  1. Consolidate bridge oversight. Transfer load certification authority to FRA under a single statutory mandate.
  2. Mandate annual inspection. Replace the current five-year cycle with twelve-month structural audits.
  3. Disclose inspection records. Require public-facing publication of every Class II bridge inspection.
  4. Tie funding to performance. Condition federal grants on documented backlog reduction by recipient.
  5. Streamline grant administration. Reduce the FAST application process from 14 stages to a single docket.
  6. Empower the Surface Transport Board. Grant STB direct enforcement powers over deferred-maintenance carriers.
  7. Fund short-line replacement. Establish a dedicated $2B annual line item for sub-Class I operators.
  8. Codify hazmat routing rules. Prohibit hazardous traffic on bridges rated structurally deficient.
