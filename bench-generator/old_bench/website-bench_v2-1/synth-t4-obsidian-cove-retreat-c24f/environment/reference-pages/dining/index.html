Culinary Experiences

A Table by the Volcano

Two distinct restaurants chart the island's culinary landscape — from a cinematic fine-dining room above the cove to barefoot grilling on the black-sand shore.

CALDERA · INTERIOR

Caldera

Fine Dining · Pacific Rim

Perched on the rim of the resort's dormant crater, Caldera serves a nightly seven-course tasting drawn from the islands of the Pacific Rim. Chef Mira Tanaka weaves yuzu, smoked dashi, and volcanic salt into precise, painterly courses. The dining room is intimate, candlelit, and reserved for evening service only.

THE TIDELINE · BEACH

The Tideline

Open-Air · Seafood Grill

Toes in the sand, fire on the shore. The Tideline is our open-air grill, where the morning's catch meets driftwood embers under a canopy of stars. The menu shifts each tide, guided by the divers and fishermen who land their boats just metres from your table. Lunch and dinner are served daily.

The Cellar at Obsidian

Both restaurants share a single subterranean cellar carved into volcanic rock, holding rare verticals from Burgundy, Central Otago, and a quiet library of Japanese ginjos. Our sommelier team curates flights nightly, or arranges private tastings deep within the cellar for resident guests.
