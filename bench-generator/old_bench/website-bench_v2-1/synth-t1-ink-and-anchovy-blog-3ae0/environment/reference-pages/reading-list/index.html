Reading List

An ongoing, honest account of what is on the bedside table, what made it through to the end, and what was set down without ceremony. Updated when the stack shifts.

Currently Reading

Letters from a Stoic — Seneca

Reread in the evenings, one letter at a time; the section on simple suppers keeps pulling me back.

Honey from a Weed — Patience Gray

Less a cookbook than a wandering field journal of Apulia, Carrara, and Naxos; I keep marking the herb chapters.

The Leopard — Giuseppe Tomasi di Lampedusa

Slow going, in the best sense; the dinner scenes alone could justify another month with it.

Finished This Year

Salt: A World History — Mark Kurlansky

Read mostly on the Hurtigruten between Bergen and Kirkenes; the chapters on garum sent me down a long anchovy detour.

Meditations — Marcus Aurelius (Hammond translation)

The Hammond is plainer than the Hays, and I think the better companion for a rainy week indoors.

The Food of Sicily — Mary Taylor Simeti

Practical, generous, and quietly opinionated; the caponata variations alone earned it a permanent shelf spot.

A Time of Gifts — Patrick Leigh Fermor

Finished on a long train from Oslo; a useful reminder that walking is still the proper speed for noticing things.

The Odyssey — Emily Wilson translation

Bright, brisk, and full of meals; I keep dog-earing the feasting passages for an essay I have not written yet.

Abandoned

Infinite Jest — David Foster Wallace

Picked it up in February, set it down in March; the footnotes won and I conceded gracefully.

On the Nature of Things — Lucretius

A poor translation soured the experience; I will try again with a different edition next winter.

The Physiology of Taste — Brillat-Savarin

Charming in small doses, exhausting at length; I prefer to keep it open on the desk and dip in.
