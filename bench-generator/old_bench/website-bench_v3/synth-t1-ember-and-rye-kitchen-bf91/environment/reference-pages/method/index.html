The Method Six Steps, Three Hours, One Pot

  1. Sear: Pat the short ribs dry, season generously with kosher salt and cracked black pepper, then brown on all sides in a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high heat until a deep mahogany crust forms — about three minutes per face.
  2. Sweat: Lower the heat and add diced yellow onion, carrot, and celery to the rendered fat; cook gently for eight minutes until the vegetables turn translucent and faintly sweet around the edges.
  3. Deglaze: Pour in the smoked rye whiskey, scraping the fond from the bottom of the pot with a wooden spoon, and let the alcohol cook off for two minutes before the liquid reduces by half.
  4. Braise: Return the ribs to the pot, add bone broth and a sprig of thyme until the meat is two-thirds submerged, cover, and slide into a 300°F oven for three hours, turning the ribs once at the midpoint.
  5. Reduce: Transfer the ribs to a warm platter, strain the braising liquid into a saucepan, and simmer hard for twelve to fifteen minutes until it coats the back of a spoon like a loose syrup.
  6. Glaze: Whisk a final tablespoon of rye and a knob of cold butter into the reduction, spoon it lavishly over the ribs, and rest for five minutes before serving with the pan juices alongside.

Active vs. Passive Time Roughly forty-five minutes of hands-on work; the remaining three hours belong to the oven and a quiet kitchen.
