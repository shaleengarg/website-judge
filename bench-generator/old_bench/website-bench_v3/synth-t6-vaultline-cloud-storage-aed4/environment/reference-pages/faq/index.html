01 Billing

How does Vaultline calculate monthly storage charges?
Storage is metered hourly and billed at the end of each calendar month. We sample the total provisioned bytes once per hour, average those samples across the billing period, and multiply by your plan's per-GB rate. Egress and API requests are billed separately and itemized on a single PDF invoice delivered to all designated billing contacts on the first business day of the month.
What happens when I exceed my plan's included storage cap?
All five plans accept overage rather than throttling writes. Each plan has a published overage rate (see table below), and overages are billed in 1 GB increments. If your overage exceeds 30% of the included cap for two consecutive months, your account manager will be notified to recommend a tier upgrade.

Do you accept purchase orders and net-60 terms?
Yes, for Enterprise and Sovereign tiers. We accept POs from organizations with a verifiable Dun & Bradstreet record, and standard payment terms are net-30. Net-60 is available on contracts of $250,000 USD or greater per year. ACH, SWIFT, and SEPA wires are all supported; credit-card billing is capped at $25,000 per month per account.
Can I get itemized billing per cost-center or project?
Yes. Apply tags of the form cost-center=cc-1234 or project=alpha to any bucket or namespace, and Vaultline will produce a per-tag subtotal on the invoice as well as a downloadable CSV breakdown via the billing console. Up to 64 distinct tag values are supported per account on Business and above.

02 Data Residency

Which regions can I pin my data to?
Vaultline operates eight named regions: US-East (Ashburn), US-West (Hillsboro), EU-West (Dublin), EU-Central (Frankfurt), AP-South (Mumbai), AP-Northeast (Tokyo), SA-East (São Paulo), and AU-Sydney. Each bucket is pinned to exactly one home region at creation time, and the home region cannot be changed in-place; instead, use the migration tooling described in section 04.
Does Vaultline replicate data across regions by default?
No. By default, data is replicated three ways within the home region only. Cross-region replication is an opt-in feature available on Scale and above, configurable per bucket with a target region, an optional one-way or two-way mode, and a replication-lag SLO of under 90 seconds for objects under 1 GB.
Can I guarantee that EU customer data never leaves the EU?
Yes. Enable the "EU-only" residency lock on your account, which prevents bucket creation in non-EU regions and blocks any administrative read access from staff workstations outside EU member states. The Sovereign tier extends this with on-shore key custody, EU-resident operations staff, and a contractually binding data-locality clause attested annually under ISO 27018.
EU-only residency lock is account-wide and cannot be partially applied. If your organization mixes EU and non-EU workloads, create separate Vaultline organizations linked under a single billing group.

03 Security & Compliance

Which certifications does Vaultline hold?
Vaultline maintains current attestations for SOC 2 Type II, SOC 3, ISO/IEC 27001, ISO/IEC 27017, ISO/IEC 27018, PCI-DSS Level 1, HIPAA (with BAA available on Enterprise and Sovereign), and FedRAMP Moderate. CSA STAR Level 2 and C5 (Germany) are available on Sovereign-tier deployments. Audit reports are downloadable via the Trust Portal under NDA.
Who holds the encryption keys to my data?
Every object is encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM with envelope encryption. By default, Vaultline manages the data encryption keys (DEKs) and the key encryption keys (KEKs) in our managed HSM. On Enterprise and Sovereign, you can supply your own KEK via Bring-Your-Own-Key (BYOK), or operate Hold-Your-Own-Key (HYOK) where keys never leave your customer-controlled HSM and Vaultline must request a wrap/unwrap operation for every read.
Are vulnerability scans and pen-tests permitted?
Yes, no advance notice is required for non-disruptive scans against your own endpoints. For load-generating or DDoS-style tests, file a notification at least 5 business days in advance via the support portal. Third-party penetration tests of the Vaultline control plane itself are not permitted; instead, customers may consume the most recent CREST-certified pen-test report under NDA.

04 Migration

How do I migrate from S3, Azure Blob, or GCS?
Use Vaultline Mover, a managed ingest service that streams objects in parallel from any S3-compatible, Azure Blob, or Google Cloud Storage source. Mover preserves object metadata, content hashes, and version history where present. For data sets above 50 TB, we also offer Vaultline Crate—a physical 96 TB encrypted appliance shipped to your data center via insured carrier.
Is there a deprecated migration path I should avoid?
The legacy vaultline-sync v1 CLI is deprecated and will be removed in Q3 2025. Deprecated If you have existing scripts that call vlsync, please migrate to vlmover which supports resumable transfers, server-side checksums, and parallel listing.

05 SLA & Uptime

What uptime do you commit to?
Starter is offered at 99.9%, Business at 99.95%, Scale and Enterprise at 99.99%, and Sovereign at 99.995%. SLAs are measured per region per calendar month against the public status page and exclude scheduled maintenance windows announced 14 days in advance.
How are SLA credits calculated and applied?
If the monthly uptime falls below the committed target, you may file a claim within 30 days. Credits are issued as a percentage of the monthly bill: 10% credit for 99.0%–99.89%, 25% for 95.0%–98.99%, and 50% for anything below 95%. Credits apply to the following invoice and cannot be redeemed for cash.

06 Performance

What throughput should I expect for large sequential reads?
Single-stream reads typically peak at 240 MB/s on Business, 480 MB/s on Scale, and 920 MB/s on Enterprise and Sovereign. Aggregate per-bucket throughput scales linearly with the number of parallel connections up to the published per-bucket cap. Refer to the Benchmark Data page for region-by-region measurements.
Are there hot-key or prefix-rate limits I should plan for?
Yes. To prevent noisy-neighbor effects, Vaultline applies an adaptive per-prefix rate limit that warms from 3,500 requests/second to over 35,000 requests/second within 90 seconds of sustained traffic. Distribute your object keys with high-entropy prefixes (e.g., a hashed account ID) to avoid hitting the soft limit on cold prefixes.

07 Access & Identity

Do you support SSO and SCIM?
SSO via SAML 2.0 and OIDC is supported on Business and above. SCIM 2.0 provisioning is supported on Scale and above and integrates with Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, JumpCloud, and OneLogin. Just-in-time provisioning is enabled by default for SAML; group-to-role mapping is configured through the admin console.
How granular are IAM policies?
Vaultline IAM policies bind principals to actions at the bucket, prefix, or object level, and policies may include conditions on source IP, request time, MFA presence, encryption mode, and object tags. Policy evaluation is deny-by-default with explicit allow statements; conflicting statements resolve in favor of deny.

08 API & SDKs

Which SDKs are officially supported?
First-party SDKs are published for Go, Python, Java, Node.js, .NET, Rust, and Ruby. All SDKs share a common configuration model and target the v3 API. The HTTP API is also S3-compatible for the most common verbs (PUT, GET, HEAD, DELETE, ListObjectsV2, multipart upload), which lets you point most existing S3 tools at Vaultline by changing only the endpoint URL.

09 Support

What are the support response times by plan?
Starter includes community and email support with best-effort responses. Business adds business-hour ticket support (P1: 4 hours). Scale adds 24×7 chat (P1: 1 hour). Enterprise adds 24×7 phone with a named Technical Account Manager (P1: 15 minutes). Sovereign adds an on-call solutions engineer in your region's working hours.
Can I get architecture review sessions?
Yes. Scale customers receive one architecture review per quarter. Enterprise and Sovereign include monthly reviews plus a yearly resilience-testing engagement that simulates region failure, key-rotation events, and identity-provider outages against a non-production copy of your environment.

10 Deprecation Policy

How much notice will I get before an API or feature is removed?
Vaultline commits to a minimum of 12 months of notice before any breaking API change or feature removal on Business and above (24 months on Enterprise and Sovereign). Deprecations are flagged in the changelog, in the response headers of affected endpoints (X-Vault-Deprecated: true), and via an email to all account owners and security contacts.
The v1 API and the vaultline-sync CLI will be permanently removed on 2025-09-30. Migrate to v3 well before that date.

11 Sovereign Tier

What makes Sovereign different from Enterprise?
Sovereign deployments run on dedicated hardware in customer-nominated facilities or partner data centers, with operations staff resident in the same legal jurisdiction as the data. Key custody is fully customer-controlled (HYOK), and Vaultline employees have no standing access; every administrative action requires a customer-signed JIT ticket. Sovereign also supports air-gapped deployments with offline update bundles.

12 Contracts & Trials

Is there a free trial?
Yes. New accounts receive a 30-day Business-tier trial including 1 TB of storage and 100 GB of egress, with no credit card required. Trials auto-convert to the Starter plan at expiry unless you upgrade. Enterprise and Sovereign do not offer self-service trials but can be evaluated through a paid proof-of-concept arrangement with your account team.
