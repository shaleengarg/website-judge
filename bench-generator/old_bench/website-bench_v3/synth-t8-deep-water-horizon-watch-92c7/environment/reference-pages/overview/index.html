A Tethys Investigation · 2019 — 2024

Operation Phantom Net: Five Years of Silence in the South Coral Sea

An eighteen-month forensic reconstruction of illegal deep-sea trawling across eleven jurisdictions, drawing on satellite AIS data, leaked vessel logs, and on-the-ground reporting from four port cities.

Executive Summary

A fleet that doesn't exist, fishing waters no one watches.

Between January 2019 and March 2024, an estimated 847 industrial trawlers operated without authorization across the deep shelves of the South Coral Sea, dragging weighted bottom gear across 12,400 square kilometres of seabed habitat. Tethys correspondents have reconstructed the scale and machinery of this fleet from leaked port records, AIS dark-period analysis, and confidential interviews with thirty-one crew members repatriated through Rabaul, Cairns, and Manila.

The vessels — registered variously to flag-of-convenience states, dissolved shell companies, and at least three sovereign fisheries authorities — landed an estimated 94,000 tonnes of unreported bycatch over the five-year window. The catch was processed at sea, transshipped under cover of night, and laundered through licensed reefer fleets into European, East Asian, and North American supply chains.

Regulatory response has been minimal. Despite eleven implicated nations and ratified obligations under UNCLOS and WCPFC, only three prosecutions have advanced to verdict, and combined fines levied across the period total US$220,000 — a figure smaller than the daily operating cost of a single mid-size trawler. Internal correspondence reviewed by Tethys suggests enforcement agencies in at least four jurisdictions were aware of vessel movements but lacked patrol assets, political authorization, or both.

This report compiles the evidentiary record. It is presented in four parts: a navigable data atlas of incident zones and vessel profiles; field dispatches from three port cities; a policy matrix benchmarking the four primary regulatory bodies; and a methodological statement detailing our sourcing, redaction practices, and ethical commitments to interviewed subjects.

Methodology Note

How We Investigated

Sources & Verification

Tethys correspondents conducted thirty-one interviews across four port cities between September 2022 and February 2024. Vessel movements were cross-referenced against AIS feeds purchased from Global Fishing Watch and triangulated with commercial satellite imagery from two independent providers during identified dark periods.

Where vessel identities could not be confirmed to a 95% confidence threshold, we have either omitted them entirely or flagged them as "suspected" in the Data Atlas. Coordinates have been rounded to the nearest 0.1° where greater precision would jeopardize ongoing prosecutions or the safety of informants.

Ethics & Redaction

No interviewed crew member is named in this report. Where direct quotation appears, vessel and date are disclosed only when the source has provided written consent and is no longer aboard the named vessel. Three sources requested full anonymity; their testimony is corroborated by at least one independent document.

This investigation received no funding from any state actor, fisheries body, or commercial seafood entity. A complete funding disclosure and a list of cited primary documents are available in the About the Investigation section.
