About

I'm Eli Hartwell. I write software for a living and essays for the rest of it. Margins is where I think out loud about the things that don't fit cleanly into a commit message: the slow work of paying attention, what it means to finish something, and why the best tools are often the ones that get out of the way.

I live in a small apartment with too many books and a kettle that whistles off-key. Before this, I spent eight years at a research lab and another four building tools for writers. I publish here when I have something to say, which is usually about once a month. If you find a typo, I would rather know than not.
