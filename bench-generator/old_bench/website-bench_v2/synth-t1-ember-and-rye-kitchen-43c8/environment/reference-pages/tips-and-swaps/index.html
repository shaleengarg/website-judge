A few honest adjustments from the test kitchen — for the nights when the pantry, the clock, or the company calls for something a little different.

Four Things Worth Knowing

Bourbon for Rye. If rye whiskey isn't on the shelf, a good bourbon will step in cleanly. Expect a softer, sweeter finish with caramel and vanilla notes — pull back the brown sugar by a teaspoon to keep the glaze from tipping into dessert territory.

Dutch Oven vs. Slow Cooker. A heavy enameled Dutch oven at 325°F is the gold standard for 3 hours 30 minutes. In a slow cooker, sear the ribs in a skillet first, then transfer everything in and cook on low for 7 to 8 hours, or high for 4 to 5. Skim the fat before glazing — there will be more of it.

Overnight Rest. If you have the patience, cool the ribs in their braising liquid and refrigerate overnight. The fat solidifies on top for easy removal, the meat tightens slightly, and the flavor deepens noticeably. Reheat gently in the liquid before applying the glaze.

Gluten-Free Thickener. Skip any flour dusting on the seared ribs and instead finish the reduced glaze with a small slurry of arrowroot or cornstarch (1 teaspoon to 1 tablespoon cold water). Whisk in off the heat — it will gloss the glaze without dulling its color.
