Winter 2024 · Anthology No. 17

The Cartographer's Elegy

a winter gathering of eighteen poems

From the editor's note This season's anthology came together under shorter days and longer rivers. Each of the eighteen poems gathered here tends, in its own register, to the difficult arithmetic of loss measured against landscape — coastlines redrawn, snowfields remembered, the small mercies of an accurate map.

A poem can be a topographic instrument. It measures what we have lost by naming, with care, what remains. — Hadley Renn, Editor
