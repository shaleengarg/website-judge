The Poets

Winter 2024 · The Cartographer's Elegy · Twelve contributors

The voices gathered for our Winter Elegy anthology write from twelve different shorelines, valleys, and weather-worn rooms. What follows is a brief directory — hometowns, a single sentence each, and the count of poems included in this issue.

  • MO
    Mirela Okonkwo
    Lagos, Nigeria · Reykjavík, Iceland
    A hydrologist by training whose poems chart the unstable border between memory and floodwater.
    3poems
  • JH
    Joaquín Heras-Vidal
    Valparaíso, Chile
    Writes elegies from the deck of a decommissioned ferry he restored with his late father.
    2poems
  • SR
    Saoirse Ruadhán
    Galway, Ireland
    Translates between bog-Latin and the dialect of her grandmother, a midwife on the Aran Islands.
    1poem
  • TK
    Tomiko Karasawa
    Sapporo, Japan
    Composes brief, snow-haunted sequences alongside her practice as a cartographer of disappearing trails.
    2poems
  • AB
    Adelaide Brockman
    Asheville, North Carolina
    Her work follows the slow grief of Appalachian streambeds rerouted by twentieth-century engineers.
    1poem
  • NE
    Nessa El-Khoury
    Beirut, Lebanon
    Writes from the threshold of her family's olive grove, returned to after fifteen years away.
    2poems
  • DV
    Dovid Volynsky
    Kraków, Poland
    A retired archivist whose late poems read like marginalia recovered from a flooded library.
    1poem
  • PA
    Pareesa Ahmadi
    Isfahan, Iran · Toronto, Canada
    Her debut sequence maps the migratory routes of cranes alongside those of her own diasporic family.
    2poems
  • RM
    Rónán McAuliffe
    Cape Breton, Nova Scotia
    Lighthouse keeper turned linguist; writes the cold light of the North Atlantic into every line.
    1poem
  • YO
    Yael Oduya
    Nairobi, Kenya
    A botanist whose poems catalogue the small extinctions occurring at the edges of the Great Rift.
    2poems
  • EL
    Elspeth Lindqvist
    Gotland, Sweden
    Writes terse, mineral poems composed mostly during the long blue hours between November and March.
    1poem
  • CB
    Cassian Bélanger
    Montréal, Québec
    Their work braids river-survey notebooks with letters to a brother who never returned from the boreal forest.
    2poems
