The Lumina Method

From Sketch to Suspended Light

Four deliberate stages shape every commission — a months-long conversation between intention, material, and the architecture that will hold the finished work.

A Four-Step Practice

How a Commission Comes to Light

Each project moves through the same four chapters, scaled to the ambition of the space — from a single foyer pendant to a 40-foot atrium screen.

01
Discovery

Concept & Sketch

We visit the site, study the light, and meet the people who will live with the work. Charcoal sketches and small watercolor studies establish form, scale, and the emotional register of the piece.

02
Specification

Material Trials

Small test pulls are produced in the studio to lock in color temperature, opacity, and the precise way each panel will refract afternoon sun. Samples are couriered for in-room approval.

03
Fabrication

Hot Shop & Cold Work

Pieces are blown, cast, or kiln-formed at our Foundry Lane studio, then cold-worked over weeks of grinding, polishing, and edge-finishing. Every component is signed and catalogued.

04
Delivery

Install & Steward

Our team crates, transports, and installs on-site alongside your architect and trades. A ten-year stewardship program covers cleaning protocols, re-tensioning, and panel replacement.

"Every commission begins with listening — to the room, to the light, and to the client who will spend a decade beneath it."
— Mara Holvenstot, Founder
