Twelve creatures,
five impossible zones.

From sunlight-stitched plankton to the ghost-pale snailfish drifting at 8,000 metres, ocean life is organized by pressure, darkness, and chemistry. This radial atlas maps twelve representative species to their native depth ring.

Bioluminescence

~90% of organisms in the twilight zone produce their own light — a chemical signature unmatched on land.

Cold Adaptation

Deep-sea fish carry antifreeze glycoproteins; their cell membranes stay fluid at −1.8°C near hydrothermal margins.

Endemism

Hadal trenches host species found nowhere else — each trench an evolutionary island isolated by ridges.
