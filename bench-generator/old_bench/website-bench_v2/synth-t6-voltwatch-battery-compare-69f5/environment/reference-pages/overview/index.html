Compare Every Watt.

VoltWatch is an independent reference for evaluating modern energy-storage cells across the three chemistries currently shaping consumer, marine, and grid-scale deployments: lithium nickel-manganese-cobalt oxide (Li-ion NMC), lithium iron phosphate (LFP), and emerging solid-state batteries. Our matrix covers fourteen production-grade models from eleven manufacturers and is updated quarterly against verified datasheets.

NMC cells continue to dominate where gravimetric energy density matters most — typically 200–270 Wh/kg — making them the default for EV conversions and weight-constrained marine systems. The tradeoff is a narrower thermal window and a cycle life that rarely exceeds 2,000 deep cycles. LFP chemistry has displaced NMC in stationary residential solar storage thanks to its remarkable thermal stability, 3,000–6,000-cycle endurance at 80% depth-of-discharge, and absence of cobalt in the cathode lattice.

Solid-state designs, still in early commercial release, promise non-flammable electrolytes, higher operating voltages, and densities exceeding 400 Wh/kg — but at price points roughly four times that of mature LFP packs. VoltWatch surfaces these tradeoffs through normalized metrics: cost-per-kWh, levelized cost-per-cycle, and a composite safety index drawn from IEC 62619 and UL 1973 test data.
