Reading List

I keep a running ledger of every book I open in a given year, partly out of librarian habit and partly because I never trust my memory of a book until I've written something down about it. The notes below are short on purpose — a sentence is usually all a book asks of me, and all I can manage between midnight and the second pot of tea.

Finished — 14 books

  • Sjón, The Blue Fox A book the size of a pamphlet that somehow contains an entire winter.
  • Ólafsdóttir, Miss Iceland Read in two sittings; underlined nothing, which is its own kind of praise.
  • Jansson, The Summer Book I keep returning to the chapter about the magic forest and forgetting why I stopped.
  • Solnit, A Field Guide to Getting Lost Made me want to take longer walks and own fewer maps.
  • Berger, And Our Faces, My Heart, Brief as Photos A book that behaves like a long letter from someone who already knows you.
  • Indriðason, Jar City Crime fiction as municipal record-keeping, which is to say: perfect.
  • Bachelard, The Poetics of Space Reread for the attics; stayed for the drawers.

Abandoned — 4 books

  • Knausgård, My Struggle, Book Four Set it down in April and have not missed it once, which feels diagnostic.
  • Foster Wallace, The Pale King Excellent on boredom; defeated me through demonstration.
  • Murakami, Killing Commendatore Quit at page 210, in the well, with no plan to return.
  • Eco, The Prague Cemetery Borrowed from a friend who will, I suspect, not ask after it.

Currently Reading — 3 books

  • Ferrante, The Days of Abandonment A book I can only read in daylight, which is rationing me to weekends.
  • Macfarlane, Underland Slow going; I keep stopping to look up words for kinds of darkness.
  • Smith, Autumn Started it in October out of sheer obedience to the title.
