About the Summit

A forum forged in the
industrial heart of America.

Ironclad Futures Summit convenes engineers, legislators, and economists each October to chart the next decade of American manufacturing — frankly, technically, and on the record.

Our Mission

Founded in 2019 by the Rivers & Rails Policy Institute, Ironclad Futures was built to close the gap between the people who write industrial policy and the people who execute it on the shop floor. Each year, the Summit produces a publicly available proceedings volume and a short list of legislative recommendations sent to the House Committee on Science, Space, and Technology.

We do not believe manufacturing's future is a foregone conclusion. We believe it is the product of deliberate choices about capital, labor, energy, and trade — choices best debated in the same room by the people who carry their consequences.

Why Pittsburgh

Pittsburgh was selected as the Summit's permanent host city in 2021. No other American metro carries the same combined inheritance of heavy industry, university-driven robotics research, and post-industrial reinvention. Within a four-mile radius of our venue you will find an active U.S. Steel works, the Carnegie Mellon Robotics Institute, three Class I rail yards, and the headquarters of two Fortune 500 industrial firms. We chose Pittsburgh because the questions we ask here are not abstract — they are paved into the streets outside.
