Le Menu du Soir

Seven Courses · Autumn Waning

A late October frost has settled across the Willamette, and with it arrives the season's quiet inventory: pumpkin still warm from the field, hazelnuts cracked the morning of, fallow venison from a single Yamhill ranch. This evening's menu is composed in deference to that frost — each plate is a small confession to one ingredient permitted to speak before the others. Chef Morin has held the techniques deliberately restrained, allowing fire, salt, and time to do the work that flourish cannot.

PREMIÈRE ASSIETTE

Huître, Pomme Verte, Genièvre

Netarts Bay oyster dressed in a clarified granny smith consommé with crushed juniper. Served on its half-shell over crushed obsidian, finished with a single drop of cold-pressed fennel oil.

DEUXIÈME ASSIETTE

Velouté de Potiron Brûlé

Sugar pumpkin from Sauvie Island roasted whole over applewood until its skin blackens, then passed through fine tamis with cultured cream. Poured tableside over toasted pepita praline and a thread of brown butter.

TROISIÈME ASSIETTE

Foie Gras, Coing, Brioche

A torchon of foie gras aged eleven days in Sauternes and white pepper, sliced cool against the grain. Accompanied by membrillo of slow-poached quince and a warm, hand-torn brioche feuilletée.

QUATRIÈME ASSIETTE

Truite de Rivière, Beurre Noisette

Deschutes River trout cured in beet salt for forty minutes, then seared skin-side only in a black steel pan. Glazed with hazelnut brown butter and a brunoise of green apple and lovage stem.

CINQUIÈME ASSIETTE

Cuisse de Pintade, Champignons des Bois

Guinea hen leg confited in its own fat with bay and clove, then crisped à la minute. Plated beside chanterelles foraged near Mount Hood and a reduction of vin jaune and shallot.

SIXIÈME ASSIETTE

Chevreuil, Cassis, Céleri-Rave

Saddle of Yamhill fallow venison aged twenty-one days, roasted rare over vine cuttings. Served with celeriac purée mounted with crème fraîche and a sauce poivrade darkened with blackcurrant.

SEPTIÈME ASSIETTE

Poire Belle-Hélène, Réinventée

Comice pear poached in barrel-aged Armagnac and Tahitian vanilla, sliced into translucent leaves. Layered with crémeux of single-origin Madagascar chocolate and a brittle of caramelized cacao nib.

The menu changes with each new moon. Dietary accommodations require seventy-two hours' advance notice and cannot be honored at the table.
