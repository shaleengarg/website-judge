Reading List

Ten books that shaped how I climb, walk, and think. Revised slowly, never in bulk.

Mountains

  • Mountains of the Mind · Robert Macfarlane The book that taught me peaks are first imagined before they are climbed — geology folded into longing.
  • Annapurna · Maurice Herzog A reminder that ambition and ruin can share the same rope, and that summits are rarely the story.
  • Touching the Void · Joe Simpson I reread it whenever I forget how stubborn a body can be against arithmetic.
  • The Living Mountain · Nan Shepherd She walks the Cairngorms inward rather than upward; it reordered my sense of what a range is for.

Philosophy

  • A Philosophy of Walking · Frédéric Gros A quiet defense of slowness — useful on long approaches and longer winters.
  • Meditations · Marcus Aurelius Pocket-sized weather for the mind; I carry the Hays translation in every summit pack.
  • The Practice of the Wild · Gary Snyder Snyder makes attention itself feel like a discipline, which is what high places ask of us anyway.

Fiction

  • Solaris · Stanisław Lem A novel about how poorly we read landscapes that do not flatter us — endlessly applicable to glaciers.
  • The Snow Leopard · Peter Matthiessen Travel as grief and grief as travel; the rare book that earns its silences.
  • Housekeeping · Marilynne Robinson No mountains in it, and yet everything I know about cold light and transience seems to live in its sentences.
