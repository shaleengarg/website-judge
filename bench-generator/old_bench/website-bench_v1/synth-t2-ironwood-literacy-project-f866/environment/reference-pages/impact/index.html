Stories from Our Learners

Behind every number is a kitchen table, a library back room, or a church basement where someone decided this was the year.

"I'd hidden it my whole working life. At fifty-three, I read my granddaughter her first bedtime story. She didn't know it was my first too."

Marlene H. — Letcher County, KY

"The tutor met me at the gas station after my shift. No judgment, just the alphabet and a thermos of coffee. Eight months later I passed my CDL written exam."

Dewey R. — McDowell County, WV

"I always signed my name with an X. Now I read the labels on my own prescriptions. That alone felt like getting my dignity back."

Eunice T. — Wise County, VA

"Ironwood didn't just teach me to read — they trained me to tutor. I run the Tuesday group at the firehall now. Three of my neighbors are reading too."

Jamie C. — Mingo County, WV

About Ironwood

The Ironwood Literacy Project is a community-rooted nonprofit providing free adult reading programs across rural Appalachia. We believe literacy is a doorway to dignity, opportunity, and connection.
