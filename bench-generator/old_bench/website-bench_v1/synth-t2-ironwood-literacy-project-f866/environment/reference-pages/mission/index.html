The Ironwood Literacy Project

Reading changes everything — one adult at a time.

Founded in 2018 in a converted feed store in Buchanan County, Virginia, Ironwood began with two retired schoolteachers, a folding table, and seven neighbors who wanted to read to their grandchildren. Six years later, we operate across fourteen counties of central Appalachia, partnering with churches, libraries, and union halls to bring adult literacy instruction to the hollows and county seats where it's needed most.

Our Core Values

Dignity First

Every adult who walks through our doors carries decades of skill, story, and survival. We meet learners as full people — never as problems to be solved — and structure every lesson around the lives they're already living.

Rooted in Place

We don't parachute in. Our tutors are neighbors, our classrooms are community spaces, and our reading materials draw from regional voices — from Breece Pancake to county newspapers — so that learning feels like coming home, not leaving it.

Patient, Persistent Work

Adult literacy is slow craft. We commit to learners for as long as it takes, measure progress in confidence as much as comprehension, and celebrate the quiet milestones: a permission slip signed, a recipe followed, a letter mailed home.

About Ironwood

The Ironwood Literacy Project is a community-based nonprofit providing free adult reading instruction across rural Appalachia. We believe literacy is a doorway — to work, to family, to self-determination.
