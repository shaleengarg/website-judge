Slow-Braised Lamb Shoulder with Preserved Lemon & Olives

Serves 4–6 · Prep 20 min · Cook 4 hr

A long, low braise that turns a humble shoulder into something perfumed and yielding — the meat falling from the bone into a sauce sharpened by preserved lemon and cured olives. It is, more or less, the dish I cook whenever someone I love is coming for dinner.

Why this recipe

I have been cooking versions of this lamb for nearly fifteen years, ever since a winter in Marrakech where I ate it three nights in a row and resolved to learn it properly. What you have here is the version I have settled on after dozens of attempts at home in Bristol: less fussy than a tagine, more forgiving than a roast, and almost impossible to ruin so long as you give it the time it asks for. It is the one recipe I would write down if I could only write down one.
