Cook's Notes

Notes & Variations

Substitutions

If preserved lemons are out of reach, use the zest of two unwaxed lemons plus a teaspoon of salt stirred into the braise — it won't have the same fermented depth, but it will sing. Boneless lamb shoulder works, though you'll lose some richness; reduce the cook time by forty minutes and check earlier. Green Castelvetrano olives can stand in for the more traditional violet or beldi olives, but avoid anything pre-pitted and bottled in vinegar.

Make Ahead

This dish improves overnight. Braise it a day in advance, cool the lamb in its liquid, and refrigerate the whole pot — the fat will set on top and protect the meat. To reheat without drying, lift the meat back into the loosened braise, cover tightly, and warm in a 150°C / 300°F oven for thirty to forty minutes until the centre reads steaming hot. Never microwave the shoulder; the fibres seize and the gloss is lost.

Serving Suggestions

Serve over buttered couscous or a wide shallow bowl of soft polenta to catch the braising liquor. A simple side of bitter leaves — chicory, watercress, escarole — dressed only with lemon and good oil cuts through the richness beautifully. Warm flatbread on the table is non-negotiable in my kitchen.

Wine: a Grenache-based southern Rhône — Gigondas, Vacqueyras, or a generous Côtes-du-Rhône Villages. The garrigue notes meet the preserved lemon and olives as though they were always meant to.
