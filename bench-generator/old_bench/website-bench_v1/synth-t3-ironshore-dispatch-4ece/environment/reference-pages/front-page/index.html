Inside the Quiet Reordering of the Strait: How Three Nations Are Rewriting Maritime Power

An eight-month investigation reveals coordinated naval deployments, undeclared logistics deals, and a new generation of port concessions reshaping global shipping lanes.

Documents obtained by the Dispatch show that three regional powers have been quietly consolidating influence over a 400-mile stretch of contested waters. Analysts say the changes — invisible from satellite imagery but unmistakable in shipping records — could redirect more than $1.4 trillion in annual trade by the end of the decade.
