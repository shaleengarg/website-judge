Speakers

Six operators. No keynotes-for-hire, no sponsor slots. Each is currently building a company in their respective field.

Confirmed for October 11

Marcus Vellaine

Founder & CEO, Axleframe Mobility — Detroit, MI

Marcus left a 12-year run at a Tier-1 auto supplier to start Axleframe, an embedded-software platform retrofitting commercial fleets with predictive drivetrain diagnostics. His team of nine has shipped to seven Midwest logistics yards in the last eighteen months.

Priya Anand-Holcomb

Co-Founder & CEO, Stoutline Freight

Priya runs a freight-brokerage startup focused on short-haul, sub-200-mile lanes that the national networks routinely misprice. Stoutline went from a spreadsheet to a $14M booked-revenue run rate in twenty-two months, entirely without outside capital.

Davis Okonkwo

Head of Operations, Brassledger — Small-Business Banking

Davis is the operator behind Brassledger, a payments and ledger product serving 4,800 trade-business owners across the Great Lakes region. He'll speak candidly about charge-off curves, compliance fatigue, and why most fintech is solving the wrong customer's problem.

Elena Ruszczyk

Founder, Coldfork Instruments — Industrial Sensors

Elena builds ruggedized vibration sensors for stamping presses and CNC lines out of a 3,200-square-foot shop in Hamtramck. Coldfork has thirty paying plants, a backlog through Q2, and exactly zero salespeople — a setup she's happy to dissect on stage.

Theo Marchetti

Solo Operator, Inboxgrade.io

Theo runs Inboxgrade, a deliverability-monitoring SaaS, entirely by himself from a one-bedroom in Corktown. He'll walk through the unglamorous reality of a $480K ARR business with one employee, no office, and a hard cap on customer count.

Naomi Halvorsen-Park

Co-Founder & CEO, Greenkettle Foods

Naomi is building a plant-protein fermentation company that closed an $11M Series A last spring and now operates a pilot facility in Wayne County. She'll talk frankly about the trade-offs of taking institutional money in a category where most of her peers haven't survived.
