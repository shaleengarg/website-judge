14 March

On Rereading Sebald in Spring

Returning to The Rings of Saturn after a decade, what surprises is not the melancholy, which one expected, but the patience of its digressions. Sebald earns each detour by walking, literally and grammatically. The sentences are themselves footpaths. There is something almost agricultural about how he tills a single image — a herring, a silkworm — until it yields whole histories.

02 March

A Note on the Comma

Editing a young essayist this week, I noticed how often the comma is asked to do the work of breath when the prose itself has forgotten how to inhale. Punctuation cannot rescue an unmusical line. The fix is rarely typographic; it lives a paragraph upstream, in the decision about which clause genuinely belongs to which thought.

21 February

Translation as Hospitality

Reading Jhumpa Lahiri on her Italian years, one feels translation reframed: not as transfer but as welcome. The host language must rearrange its furniture, lower its voice, give up the largest chair. Fidelity is too narrow a word. What we want from a translator is something closer to discretion — the willingness to be present without presuming.

09 February

The Long Novel Question

A correspondent asks whether the long novel is finished. I think the long novel is exactly as alive as long attention, and long attention has not vanished, only migrated. It hides in podcasts, in serialized streaming, in the way readers will spend forty hours with a video game. The form is patient. It can wait for us to come back.

28 January

Against the Tidy Ending

There is a tyranny of resolution in contemporary short fiction — the closing image that arrives just slightly too prepared, like a coat already in hand. The best endings I read this month felt instead like overhearing the next sentence of a conversation in another room. Something continues without us. The story does not so much conclude as turn its face.

15 January

Notebook of a Slow Reader

I have begun keeping two bookmarks per book: one where I am reading, one where I am thinking. They drift apart. The gap between them is, I suspect, where the actual reading happens — that pasture of unhurried disagreement with the writer. Bookstores rarely sell the second bookmark, but every serious reader has improvised one.

03 January

On Critics Who Quote Too Much

A review crowded with citation can feel generous, but the generosity is often misplaced. We came to the critic for a sentence the writer could not provide. When a review is mostly excerpt, it concedes the harder labor — the labor of judgment, which is to say of risk. A good critic borrows briefly and then pays in their own coin.

19 December

A Year of Marginalia

Reviewing the year's annotations, I find I have written the word yes in the margin two hundred and forty times, and the word no only thirty-one. This is either the sign of a generous reader or a lazy one. I prefer to call it weather: the climate of assent we bring to certain writers, the cold front we reserve for others.
