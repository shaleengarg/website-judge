About the Quarterly

A small magazine of long-form essays, conversations, and reading notes — printed four times a year in the city, mailed everywhere.

Our Founding

Inkwell Quarterly was founded in the autumn of 2014 in a narrow second-floor office above a closed bookbinder's shop, with a borrowed typewriter, three editors, and the conviction that the slow essay still had readers. The first issue ran to forty-eight pages and sold out of its modest print run within a season.

In the decade since, the magazine has remained stubbornly quarterly. We publish four issues a year on uncoated stock, each organized around a loose thematic preoccupation rather than a strict topic — restlessness, inheritance, the domestic interior, the weather of cities. We pay our writers, slowly but reliably, and we read every submission.

The masthead has grown to include contributing editors across three continents, but the editorial sensibility remains the one we began with: attention paid patiently, sentences built carefully, and a deep suspicion of the hot take.

Editorial Philosophy

We believe that criticism is a form of close attention, and that close attention is a form of care. We publish writers who are willing to be uncertain on the page, who follow a sentence to find out where it ends, and who treat the reader as a patient and curious companion rather than an audience to be persuaded.
