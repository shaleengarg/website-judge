The Current Issue · Autumn

Volume XII — The Restless City

An issue assembled in transit, drafted between platforms and late kitchens, attending to the cities that refuse stillness. We gather essays, interviews, and small notes from writers tracing the architecture of unease.

Lead Stories

FEATURED · ESSAY
Essay

The Cartography of Sleeplessness

In a long walking essay through Lisbon's nocturnal districts, Halverson maps the small economies of insomnia — the bakeries, the all-night chapels, the trams that ferry the unsettled toward dawn and a quieter species of attention.

— by Eira Halverson
DISPATCH
Dispatch

After the Last Tenant

A rent-stabilized building in Queens empties one apartment at a time. Marsh listens at the seams between leases, recording the language landlords reach for when describing what is, after all, a slow erasure.

— by Coleman Marsh
CRITICISM
Criticism

Against the Walkable City

Liang interrogates the genteel vocabulary of urbanism — walkability, vibrancy, mixed-use — and finds inside it a quiet preference for the affluent stroller and the cleanly composed photograph of leisure.

— by Wen Liang
