Our Story

— una piccola storia di famiglia —

Trattoria Belluno opened its doors in the autumn of 1991, when Maria and Giuseppe Conti arrived from the village of Belluno with little more than a suitcase, a wooden rolling pin, and a notebook of recipes that had been kept in the family since before the war. The first dining room had only eight tables, a single oven, and a hand-lettered sign in the window that read, simply, “Aperto.”

In the decades since, the neighborhood has changed many times over. The bakery across the street became a bank, then a coffee shop, then a bakery again. Through it all our doors have stayed open, our recipes have stayed honest, and our regulars — some of them now bringing their grandchildren — have stayed loyal. We are grateful for every one of them.

Today the kitchen is led by the Contis’ son, who learned to fold ravioli before he could read. We still make our pasta by hand each morning, still press our own olive oil with a cousin in Umbria, and still believe that a meal is something to be lingered over, not rushed through. Some things, we think, are worth keeping the same.

Marco Conti
Chef & Proprietor
