Special Report · Freight Infrastructure

The Iron Corridor:
Collapse in Slow Motion.

A two-year investigation into the inland freight rail network finds that 34% of Class II freight rail bridges are rated structurally deficient — and that maintenance budgets, adjusted for tonnage, have fallen every year since 2009.

By M. Halloran  ·  R. Okafor  ·  J. Petrov
Investigations Desk · 18-month inquiry
Filed  Nov 14, 2024
Read time  42 min
Part 1 of 5
Key Findings

Three Numbers
That Define the Crisis.

Drawn from FRA Form 6180.55, AAR annual returns, and field inspection records 2018–2023.
FINDING / 01 BRIDGES
1,247
Failures · 2018–2023

Bridge failures have tripled since 2010.

Federal inspection logs document 1,247 partial or complete bridge failures across short-line and regional carriers in five years. Eighty-one occurred on routes hauling hazardous materials.

FINDING / 02 INCIDENTS
8,914
Derailments · 2018–2023

One derailment every five hours, on average.

Across the inland freight network, 8,914 derailment incidents were logged in six years. Track defects — not human error — were the leading cause in 46% of cases reviewed.

FINDING / 03 SHORTFALL
$12.4B
Federal Shortfall · FY23

The gap between authorized and disbursed funds.

Of the $15.5 billion authorized for freight rail safety upgrades under the 2015 FAST Act, only $3.1 billion has reached carriers. The remainder sits in administrative limbo.

Interstitial · Chapter Marker § 00 — Preamble

This report draws on 14,000 federal inspection records and 62 field interviews conducted across 19 states between June 2023 and October 2024.
