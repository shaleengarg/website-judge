Join the Work of Conservation

Across 14,000 acres of forest, wetland, and farmland, our mission depends on neighbors, donors, and landowners who share a long view of the Appalachian landscape. There are three primary ways to take part.

VOLUNTEER

Volunteer

Our volunteer stewards are the eyes and hands on the ground. Throughout the year, crews meet for trail monitoring days along the Hollow Creek Greenway and surrounding easement preserves, walking established routes to log trail damage, downed timber, and signs of unauthorized use. We also host monthly invasive species removal workdays targeting autumn olive, multiflora rose, and Oriental bittersweet in priority habitats. No experience is required — tools, gloves, and training are provided, and most workdays run from 8 AM to 1 PM with coffee and lunch on us.

Sign Up to Volunteer
DONATE

Donate

Every easement we hold is monitored, defended, and stewarded forever — and that permanence is funded by donors who believe in long horizons. Gifts to our Annual Fund cover operating costs, monitoring visits, and community programming across the watershed. Planned giving through bequests, charitable trusts, and qualified charitable distributions allows supporters to extend that protection into the next generation. All gifts are tax-deductible, and 89 cents of every dollar goes directly to conservation work.

Make a Gift
EASEMENTS

Easement Landowners

If you own forest, farmland, or stream frontage in our service region and want to keep it intact, a voluntary conservation easement may be the right tool. An easement is a legal agreement that permanently limits subdivision and development while leaving the land in private ownership and active use. Our staff will walk your property with you, discuss your goals, and outline the appraisal, baseline documentation, and stewardship endowment steps. Initial consultations are confidential and carry no obligation.
