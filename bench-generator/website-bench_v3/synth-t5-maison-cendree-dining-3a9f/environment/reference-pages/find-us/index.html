The Cast-Iron Lantern

The mill sits at a quiet bend of the French Broad, a low stone building that gives no announcement of itself. Guests arriving for the first time often pass us once before circling back. We expect this, and we are glad for it — the approach is part of the evening.

From Downtown

Twelve minutes by car from Pack Square. Head north on Merrimon Avenue, then west across the river at the Pearson Drive bridge. Turn left onto Riverside Road and continue past the old freight depot. The drive winds along the water for roughly half a mile before the trees close in.

Route — Pack Square → Merrimon Ave N → Pearson Dr W → Riverside Rd S
Approx. 12 Minutes · 3.4 Miles

The Entrance

Our drive is unmarked. Look for a single cast-iron lantern set on a low fieldstone post on the river side of Riverside Road, lit from sundown each evening of service. The gravel lane beyond bears no signage; follow it slowly through the trees for roughly two hundred yards to the gatehouse, where our valet will meet you.

If you find yourself uncertain, do not turn back — the lantern is closer than you think.

Valet & Parking

Valet service is offered each evening of dinner service at the gatehouse, beginning one hour before seating. The fee is fifteen dollars per vehicle and is included with the cover charge on Fridays. Self-parking is not available on the mill grounds; the lane is single-track and reserved for the valet's use.

A Note on Weather

The mill stands at the river's edge, and the lane through the woods can darken quickly in fog or rain. We keep an umbrella stand at the gatehouse and a small fire in the foyer through October. Should weather make travel genuinely unsafe, telephone the host desk and we will gladly hold your evening for another date.
