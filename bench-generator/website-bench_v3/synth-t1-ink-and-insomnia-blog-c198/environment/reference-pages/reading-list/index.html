Reading List

Books I've loved, dog-eared, or learned not to open after 11pm.

Currently Reading

The Lonely City — Olivia Laing A slow, generous meditation on solitude in big cities — I keep stopping to underline things.
Stoner — John Williams A quiet novel about an unremarkable life that somehow feels enormous at 2am.
In Praise of Shadows — Jun'ichirō Tanizaki An essay about dim light and lacquer that has changed how I look at my apartment.

Favorites (Paperback Worn)

Bluets — Maggie Nelson 240 short prose fragments about the color blue, heartbreak, and obsession — the book I lend and lose most.
Jesus' Son — Denis Johnson Sentences that feel like they were written on diner napkins; everything I aspire to as a writer.
The Year of Magical Thinking — Joan Didion Grief rendered in cold, exact prose — a yearly reread when I need to remember how to be honest.
A Field Guide to Getting Lost — Rebecca Solnit Essays on uncertainty and wandering that I keep on the nightstand like a flashlight.
Cannery Row — John Steinbeck A short, warm book about misfits in a small place; the literary equivalent of a 3am booth.

Do Not Read Near Midnight

House of Leaves — Mark Z. Danielewski A novel about a house that's bigger on the inside; I made the mistake of starting it at 1am and didn't sleep for two days.
Pet Sematary — Stephen King The only King book I had to physically put in another room before turning out the light.
The Fisherman — John Langan A grief novel disguised as cosmic horror — quiet for 100 pages, then absolutely not.
Hell House — Richard Matheson Old-fashioned haunted-house dread that still works; I read it in daylight and it still followed me to bed.
The Haunting of Hill House — Shirley Jackson The first paragraph alone has kept me awake more than coffee ever has.

Ink & Insomnia — Mara Veltri · Chicago
