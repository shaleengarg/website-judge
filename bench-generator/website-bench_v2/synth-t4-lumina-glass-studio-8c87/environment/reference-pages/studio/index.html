The Studio

A practice built in heat, breath, and patience.

Founded in 1997 by Mara Holvenstot, Lumina Glass Studio occupies a converted iron foundry on Portland's east bank. Twenty-six years on, the work remains stubbornly handmade.

Mara trained under master glassblower Lino Tagliapietra in Murano before returning to the Pacific Northwest to open a single-furnace workshop in 1997. The studio has since grown to a team of seven artisans, but every commission still passes through her hands — from the first charcoal sketch to the final anneal.

Our work lives in private residences, embassies, hotel atria, and the permanent collections of three regional museums. We accept a limited number of commissions each year so that every panel receives the slow, deliberate attention it requires.

— Mara Holvenstot, Founder & Principal Artist
