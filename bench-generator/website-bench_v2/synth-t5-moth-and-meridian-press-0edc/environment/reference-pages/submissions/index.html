Spring Thaw 2025 — Reading Period

Submissions

We are now accepting work for our seventeenth seasonal anthology, Spring Thaw 2025. The reading window is brief and the editorial collective small; please read the guidelines below in full before sending poems. We respond to every submission, usually within ten weeks of the close of the window.

“We are looking for poems that know the name of the soil beneath them.”

  • 01Reading Window

    The window opens February 1, 2025 at 9:00 EST and closes March 15, 2025 at 23:59 EST. Submissions received outside this period are returned unread. We do not offer expedited or rolling consideration.

  • 02What to Send

    Up to five poems per submission, gathered in a single document. There is no line limit on individual poems, though work over three pages should justify its length. One submission per poet, per reading period.

  • 03Previously Published Work

    We consider unpublished poems only. This includes personal newsletters, public social posts, and chapbooks circulated beyond a workshop. Simultaneous submissions are welcome; please withdraw promptly if a poem is placed elsewhere.

  • 04Rights & Compensation

    Poets retain all rights to their work. Moth & Meridian requests first serial rights for the digital anthology and a non-exclusive archival license thereafter. Accepted poets receive an honorarium of $75 per poem.

  • 05Formatting

    Plain text, .docx, or .pdf — any legible serif at 11pt or larger. Include a brief third-person bio (under 80 words) and your hometown in the body of the email, not in the document itself. We read submissions blind.

  • 06What We Are Drawn To

    Poems at the intersection of grief, cartography, and natural wonder. Work that holds a landscape accountable. Translations welcome with translator's note and confirmation of permissions. We read with patience and read aloud.
