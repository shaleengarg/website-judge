About the Press

Founded 2019 · Three anthologies a year · Read everywhere there is weather

Our Origins

Moth & Meridian Press began in the autumn of 2019, around a kitchen table in a rented house at the edge of a salt marsh. The founders — three poets and a former cartographer — had been trading letters for years about the same recurring problem: the poems they most needed to read were the ones least likely to find a permanent home. Work that lingered between elegy and field notebook, between grief and the precise naming of grasses, kept slipping past the larger journals. We started the press to keep those poems lit a little longer.

The name comes from two of our shared obsessions. A moth, drawn helplessly toward what burns it. A meridian, an imaginary line that orders the whole confused earth. Poetry, we think, lives in the friction between the two.

We publish poems that hold a landscape accountable — that remember what was promised by a river, and what the river forgot.

What We Publish

Our editorial philosophy is narrow and stubborn. We are interested in poems written at the intersection of grief, cartography, and natural wonder — poems that pay attention to the world as if attention itself were a form of repair. We favor work that is slow, exact, and willing to sit with what cannot be repaired. We are uninterested in irony as a defense.

Each year we release three seasonal anthologies, edited collaboratively by the full collective. We publish emerging poets alongside established voices in the same pages, without distinction in placement or fanfare.

Spring Thaw

Released in April. Poems of return, mud, first migrations, the small violences of new growth.

Midsummer Rift

Released in August. Poems written in the bright, exhausted middle — drought, fire, longest light.

Winter Elegy

Released in December. Poems of accounting and absence; what the year carried away, and what it left.

The Editorial Collective

Moth & Meridian is run by a rotating collective of six editors, each serving a three-year term. The collective currently includes poets working from coastal Maine, the high desert of New Mexico, the Scottish Borders, a riverbank in Lagos, a small island in the Inland Sea of Japan, and a third-floor walk-up in Montréal. We meet, mostly, in the margins of long shared documents.

We pay every contributor an honorarium, however modest, because attention is labor. We read every submission ourselves, slowly, often more than once. We answer every poet who writes to us, even when the answer is no.

If you would like to submit work to a forthcoming anthology, please see our submissions page for the current reading window and guidelines.
