Chapter 04 · Crushing Forces

The weight of water.

Every ten meters of descent adds another atmosphere of pressure. At the Mariana Trench floor, the ocean presses down with the force of 1,086 atmospheres — equivalent to balancing fifty jumbo jets on a single shoulder.

Section 02 — Reference

What can survive down there?

Acrylic-hulled tourist vessels rated for shallow dives implode if taken below ~600 meters. Steel-hulled military submarines push to roughly 730m. Only titanium spheres and syntactic foam composites — like those engineered for the Deepsea Challenger — have reached the hadal floor and returned intact.

The pressure differential between a research sub's interior (1 atm) and the trench exterior (1086 atm) is one of the steepest engineered gradients on Earth.
