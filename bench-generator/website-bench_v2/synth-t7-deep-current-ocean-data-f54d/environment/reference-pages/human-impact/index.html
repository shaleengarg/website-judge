Chapter 05 — Anthropocene Ocean

The deepest place on Earth is no longer untouched.

Sixty years after humans first descended to the Mariana Trench, our footprint precedes us. Plastic, warming, and overfishing have rewritten the chemistry of every ocean layer — from sunlit reefs to the hadal floor.

A Timeline of Disturbance

Four pivotal moments where humanity met the deep — and changed it.

Metric Tons / Year
Plastic waste entering the ocean annually — equivalent to a garbage truck every minute.

Coral Reefs Lost
Since 1950, half of the world's living coral reef cover has disappeared due to warming and acidification.
90%
Large Fish Depleted
Industrial fishing has reduced populations of tuna, marlin, sharks, and cod by ninety percent in a century.
