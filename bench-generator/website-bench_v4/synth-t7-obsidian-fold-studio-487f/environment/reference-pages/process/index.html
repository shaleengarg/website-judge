— Methodology / 04 Phases

The shape of how we work.

A linear sequence applied to non-linear problems. Four phases — disciplined, recursive, and unhurried — that move from research to release.

— Sequence

From quiet excavation to the loud act of release.

Every project moves through four named phases. The boundaries are deliberate. The pace is editorial.

Phase 01

Excavation

Every identity sits atop a buried structure of history, intent, and contradiction. Excavation is the slow, listening phase: we conduct stakeholder interviews, audit archives, study competitors and adjacent disciplines, and read the cultural weather around the brand. We catalogue the artifacts of the institution — letters, signage, sketches, sounds — and surface the unspoken assumptions held by its keepers. Nothing is designed yet. Nothing is judged. We compile a research dossier of roughly fifty to eighty pages that becomes the project's shared vocabulary. By the end of this phase, we know what the brand has been, what it has refused to become, and which threads are worth pulling forward into the next.

Phase 02

Architecture

Architecture is where strategy becomes structure. We draft the positioning, articulate the brand's posture toward its audiences, and map the hierarchy of expressions — primary mark, secondary devices, typographic system, color logic, motion grammar, photographic stance. Decisions are tested against the dossier and against three or four divergent design directions, each pushed far enough to be defensible on its own. We do not present mood boards; we present working systems. The chosen direction is documented as a foundation specification — proportions, ratios, exception rules — so that everything built afterward inherits the same internal physics. This is the phase that determines whether the identity will hold.

Phase 03

Voice

A brand is its language as much as its mark. In Voice, we develop the verbal identity in lockstep with the visual: tone of address, vocabulary, sentence rhythm, the specific words refused. We write a tone-of-voice manual with worked examples — exhibition wall labels, press releases, customer service replies, founder essays — so that the brand can be heard before it is seen. We collaborate with editors and translators when the work crosses languages. Sound, where relevant, enters here too: a sonic logo, an acoustic palette, the ambient texture of a flagship space. By the end of this phase, the brand speaks in full sentences with a recognisable cadence.

Phase 04

Release

Release is the deliberate act of letting the identity meet the world. We produce final artwork, font files, motion templates, print and digital production specs, and a guidelines document built for the team that will inherit it — not for a trophy case. We rehearse the rollout: internal launch, soft external preview, full reveal, post-launch monitoring. We sit with the client through the first three months, adjusting where reality reveals what the studio could not. Release is not the end of the project; it is the moment the system becomes responsible to the people who use it. Our job is to make that handover feel inevitable.
