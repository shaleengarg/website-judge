Mira Voss

Relief Printmaker — Woodcut & Linocut, Berlin 2014–present

Focus

Mira Voss carves the slow architecture of industry: cooling towers, blast furnaces, freight yards, and the long horizontal silhouettes of the Ruhr Basin at dusk. Her relief work treats these structures as portraits rather than ruins — patient, frontal, and weighted with the kind of stillness that only ink on damp paper can hold.

Each block is drawn from direct observation, often over several visits at different hours, and is reduced through carving to the smallest number of marks that will carry the subject.

Materials

She works exclusively on long-grain cherry and shina plywood for woodcuts, and on traditional battleship linoleum for finer linework. Papers are Japanese kozo and gampi, selected sheet by sheet; pigments are water-based sumi and earth inks, mixed in the studio without commercial driers or solvents.

Editions are kept deliberately small — rarely above thirty — so that each impression remains close to the block and to the hand that pulled it.

Studio

All work is produced at Schwarzschnitt Studio, Berlin-Neukölln.
