Conversations

Six interviews from Volume XII — writers, translators, and editors on the work of making sentences in a restless season.

“To write in a second language is to keep a diary in a house you don't yet own — every verb a borrowed lamp, every silence a window left open on purpose.” — Mara Voss, in conversation with Helen Aldama
MV
Mara Voss

On Exile and Language: A Talk with Mara Voss

Interview by Helen Aldama

The novelist reflects on three decades writing between Buenos Aires and Berlin, the texture of borrowed grammar, and why she still drafts every chapter twice — first in Spanish, then again in German.

JK
Julian Kerr

The Patience of Form: Julian Kerr on the Long Poem

Interview by Naomi Strand

After fourteen years at work on a single sequence, the poet discusses the ethics of revision, the seductions of the fragment, and what it means to keep faith with an unfinished book.

AO
Adaeze Okafor

Rooms Without Doors: Adaeze Okafor on the Domestic Novel

Interview by Theo Marsh

The Lagos-born novelist on inheritance, the architecture of small apartments, and why she writes mothers who are allowed, at last, to be uninteresting on the page.

RL
Roman Lévay

Translating the Unfinished: A Dialogue with Roman Lévay

Interview by Clara Bisset

The translator of three posthumous editions speaks plainly about working from notebooks, restoring punctuation an author never settled, and the quiet violence of every editorial choice.

SN
Soraya Nadir

Criticism as Companionship: Soraya Nadir in Conversation

Interview by Peter Vance

The essayist on her decade at the literary weeklies, what reviewing taught her about disagreement, and why she now believes the most useful criticism resembles a long, attentive walk.

DH
Dmitri Halpern

The Editor's Ear: Dmitri Halpern on Forty Years in Print

Interview by Margot Iwasaki

The legendary editor recalls debut manuscripts, friendships that became books, and the small, daily kindnesses of working with writers who do not yet know what they have made.
