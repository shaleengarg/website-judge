Our Programs

Ironwood runs three active programs across rural Appalachia, each shaped by the communities they serve. From one-on-one tutoring in church basements to workplace literacy on the mill floor, our adult learners meet skilled volunteer instructors where they already are. Below are the programs currently accepting new participants.

Hearth & Home Tutoring

Serves: McDowell & Mingo Counties, WV

Schedule: Tues & Thurs evenings, 6–8pm

One-on-one weekly tutoring for adults building foundational reading skills. Sessions are held in libraries, community centers, and family homes — wherever a learner feels most comfortable.

Worksite Reading Circles

Serves: Letcher & Harlan Counties, KY

Schedule: Weekday lunch hour, 12–1pm

Small-group literacy classes hosted on-site at partnering employers. Curriculum blends everyday reading with workplace documents, safety materials, and forms learners encounter on the job.

Mountain Family Literacy

Serves: Buchanan, Dickenson & Wise, VA

Schedule: Saturdays, 10am–12pm

A parent-and-child program that pairs adult reading instruction with read-aloud time for kids. Families leave each session with a free book and shared practice routines for the week ahead.

Eligibility

Programs are free and open to adults age 18 and older living in our service counties. No prior reading level is required — we meet learners where they are. Childcare and transportation assistance are available on request.

About Ironwood

The Ironwood Literacy Project is a community-based nonprofit providing free adult reading programs across rural Appalachia. We believe every adult deserves the chance to read with confidence — and that strong readers build stronger communities.
