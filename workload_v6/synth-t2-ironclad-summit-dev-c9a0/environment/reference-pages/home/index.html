About the Summit

Ironclad Summit brings together the practitioners advancing resilient infrastructure design — from kernel-level systems work to distributed platform operations and edge hardware.

Now in its sixth year, Ironclad remains stubbornly technical. Every session is delivered by the engineers who shipped the system, ran the incident, or wrote the failure-mode analysis. No vendor keynotes, no marketing tracks — just real work, post-mortems, and the architectural decisions that hold up under load.

Join 1,200 systems engineers, SREs, platform leads, and hardware designers in Pittsburgh this October for two days of sustained technical depth and the conversations you can only have in person.
