The Featured Supper

Slow-Braised Lamb Shanks with White Bean Ragout

Four hours, one heavy pot, and a quiet kitchen. Lamb shanks fall from the bone into a bed of cannellini beans loosened with anchovy and rosemary, finished with a long, mellow pour of dry vermouth that turns everything golden at the edges.

A heavy copper pot at a slow tremor.

Braised four hours; rested ten minutes; eaten without ceremony.

Serves 4
Prep 25 min
Cook 3 hr 20 min
Start with the Pantry
Origin

From a Ligurian Kitchen

This recipe began at my grandmother Adelina's stove in a narrow flat above a butcher's shop in Genoa, where the smell of bay leaves and seared lamb seeped into the wallpaper. She braised shanks the way other women said the rosary — patiently, in the same order, with absolute faith that nothing would be wasted.

The white beans came from a paper sack on top of the cupboard, soaked overnight in a chipped enamel bowl. The anchovy was almost a secret; she would never have called it a flavour, only a salt. The dry vermouth was poured by eye, twice, the second time muttered as a blessing. What you have here is faithful to her method, translated for a domestic oven and a single quiet afternoon.

If anything is sacred in this dish, it is the hour after the lid first goes on — when the kitchen turns warm and forgiving, and there is very little left to do but trust the pot.
