Step by Step

Method

Four phases, paced so the oven does most of the work while you set the table.

Active 40 min
Hands-off 2 hr 40 min
Rest 10 min

1 · Marinating

  1. Pat the four lamb shanks dry with paper towel and season generously on all sides with fine sea salt and cracked black pepper.
  2. In a wide dish, combine 3 tbsp olive oil, 4 crushed garlic cloves, 2 sprigs rosemary, 6 anchovy fillets mashed to a paste, and the zest of one lemon.
  3. Rub the marinade into the shanks, turning to coat every surface. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, ideally overnight.
  4. Remove the shanks from the fridge 45 minutes before cooking so they come to room temperature; scrape off excess herbs but reserve them.

2 · Searing

  1. Heat a heavy enamelled Dutch oven over medium-high until just smoking. Add 2 tbsp neutral oil and swirl to coat.
  2. Sear the shanks two at a time, undisturbed, for 3–4 minutes per side until a deep mahogany crust forms. Transfer to a tray.
  3. Lower the heat to medium. Add 1 diced onion, 2 diced carrots, and 2 sliced celery ribs; cook 8 minutes until softened and lightly caramelised.
  4. Stir in 2 tbsp tomato paste and the reserved marinade herbs; cook 2 minutes until the paste darkens and smells toasted.
  5. Deglaze the pot with 120 ml dry vermouth, scraping up all browned bits with a wooden spoon. Simmer 90 seconds to burn off the alcohol.

3 · Braising

  1. Heat the oven to 150°C (300°F). Nestle the seared shanks back into the pot in a single layer, bone-end up where possible.
  2. Pour in 500 ml lamb or chicken stock, just enough to come two-thirds up the shanks. Tuck in 2 bay leaves and a strip of orange peel.
  3. Bring to a bare simmer on the stovetop, then cover with a tight lid and slide into the oven. Braise undisturbed for 2 hours.
  4. Uncover, baste the tops, and continue braising for 30–40 minutes more, until the meat retreats from the bone and yields to a spoon.
  5. Lift the shanks onto a warm platter, tent loosely with foil, and rest for at least 10 minutes while you finish the ragout.

4 · Building the Ragout

  1. Skim visible fat from the braising liquid with a ladle. Reserve 1 tbsp of the rendered lamb fat for finishing.
  2. Stir 400 g cooked white beans (cannellini or coco) into the pot along with their cooking liquor or 100 ml stock to loosen.
  3. Simmer uncovered over medium-low heat for 8–10 minutes, crushing a quarter of the beans against the side of the pot to thicken the sauce.
  4. Fold in 2 tbsp chopped flat-leaf parsley, 1 tsp lemon juice, and the reserved lamb fat. Taste and adjust salt; the anchovy should whisper, not shout.
  5. Spoon the ragout into wide shallow bowls, perch a shank on top, and finish with a drizzle of good olive oil and a final crack of pepper.

Plating Note

Serve at once with crusty sourdough for mopping. Leftover ragout deepens overnight — reheat gently with a splash of stock.
