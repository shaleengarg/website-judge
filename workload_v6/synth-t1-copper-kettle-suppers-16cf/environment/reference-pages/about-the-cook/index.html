About the Cook
TM

Teodora Marchetti

Cook, gardener, lifelong defender of a long, low oven.

Teodora grew up in a third-floor flat above a fishmonger in Genoa, where the windows opened onto the smell of salt and basil and the kitchen never quite stopped working. Her grandmother kept a copper pot on the back burner from morning into evening, and the rule of the house was simple: nothing was rushed, nothing was wasted, and a beans-and-greens supper could carry a family through any kind of week.

She moved to Edinburgh in her late twenties, trading the Ligurian coast for a stone tenement and a small back garden that now grows more rosemary than it has any right to. Her cooking sits somewhere between those two places — Italian patience translated through a Scottish pantry. She writes about food the way she cooks it: slowly, plainly, and with a deep suspicion of any recipe that promises to be quick.

A good braise forgives almost everything except impatience.
