The Pantry List

What You'll Need

Gather everything before the pot meets the flame. The recipe leans on a short list of well-chosen building blocks — good shanks, dry herbs, an honest splash of vermouth — so quality matters more than quantity. Quantities below serve four generously.

Lamb & Marinade 7 items

  • 4 meaty lamb shanks, ~380 g each, trimmed of excess fat
  • 2 tbsp coarse sea salt, for the dry cure
  • 1 tbsp cracked black peppercorns
  • 6 sprigs fresh rosemary, lightly bruised
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed with the flat of a knife
  • 2 strips lemon zest, pith removed
  • 3 tbsp olive oil, for searing

White Bean Ragout 9 items

  • 350 g dried cannellini beans, soaked overnight (or 2 × 400 g tins, drained)
  • 1 large yellow onion, finely diced
  • 2 medium carrots, cut into 1 cm dice
  • 2 ribs celery, cut into 1 cm dice
  • 6 oil-packed anchovy fillets, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste, double-concentrated
  • 120 ml dry white vermouth (Noilly Prat preferred)
  • 700 ml rich lamb or chicken stock, warmed
  • 1 bay leaf and 2 sprigs thyme, tied with kitchen string

Finishing Touches 4 items

  • 1 small handful flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
  • 1 tsp finely grated lemon zest, for brightness
  • 2 tbsp good extra-virgin olive oil, for drizzling
  • — flaky sea salt and coarse black pepper, to taste

Acceptable Substitutions

No vermouth? A dry white wine such as Vermentino or Pinot Grigio will do, though the finished ragout will lose a touch of its herbaceous edge. Anchovies can be swapped for 1 tbsp white miso for a similar umami backbone. Tinned cannellini skip the overnight soak — add them in the final 30 minutes only, or they will collapse into the sauce.
