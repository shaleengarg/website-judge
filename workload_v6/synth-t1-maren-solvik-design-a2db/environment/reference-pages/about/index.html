About Maren

Norwegian-born designer, working from Berlin since 2021.

Portrait — studio, Neukölln

Biography

Maren Solvik (b. 1988, Ålesund) is a graphic designer focused on brand identity and print work for cultural institutions. She studied visual communication at Oslo National Academy of the Arts (KHiO), graduating with distinction in 2012 after a thesis on Norwegian municipal wayfinding systems.

For seven years she lived and worked in Bergen, first as a junior designer at a small studio serving regional museums and theatres, then as an independent designer working with publishers, festivals, and a long-running collaboration with Bergen Kunsthall. The west-coast years shaped her approach: slow research, careful typography, and a preference for printed matter that can be held.

Since 2021 she has run a one-person studio in Berlin, taking on a small number of identity, publication, and exhibition projects each year for clients across Northern Europe. She lectures occasionally at HAW Hamburg and is a member of Forening Norske Grafiske Designere. Outside the studio she keeps a Risograph, a stubborn sourdough, and a habit of walking the same canal route every morning.
