How I Work

Every project begins with reading, listening, and looking — long before a single line is drawn. My approach is research-first, treating each brief as a question to be understood rather than a problem to be solved quickly.

Stage01 Discovery

I start with conversations and archives. For cultural clients this often means visiting the institution, reading its history, and meeting the people who keep it running. The goal of discovery is not to gather references but to find the specific tone, audience, and constraints that should shape every decision that follows.

Stage02 Concept Development

From the research I draft two or three distinct directions, each anchored in a clear written rationale rather than a mood board. Sketches stay rough on purpose. I want clients to respond to the underlying idea — the typographic temperament, the structural logic, the colour argument — before we commit to surface.

Stage03 Refinement

One direction is chosen and developed in depth. This is where typography is set properly, grids are pressure-tested across formats, and print samples come back from the press for review. I prefer to refine in real materials whenever possible: risograph proofs, paper mock-ups, full-scale signage tests.

Stage04 Delivery

Final files are prepared for every required output — print-ready PDFs, layered source files, and a concise brand manual written for the people who will use it daily. I stay involved through the first production run, attending press checks when budgets allow, and remain on hand for questions long after sign-off.

If this way of working sounds like a fit for your institution or imprint, I'd be glad to hear about your project. I take on a small number of new commissions each year and respond to every inquiry personally.
