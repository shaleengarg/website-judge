Plastic Tides

A polymer cartography of the open sea — tracing 11 million metric tons of plastic from the moment they leave the shore to the gyres, fibres, and microparticles that now define modern marine geology.

From single-use packaging to ghost gear, every tide carries a synthetic ledger. This issue maps the inputs, the flows, and the polymer chemistry of an ocean increasingly composed of human residue.

§ 01 / Gyre Mass Atlas

Five Spinning Archives

Updated · Q3 2024

Five subtropical gyres — vast rotational currents driven by the Coriolis effect — concentrate buoyant debris into convergence zones the size of subcontinents. The North Pacific accumulation alone now contains an estimated 79,000 MT of floating plastic spread across 1.6 million km².

§ 02 / Source Provenance

Where the Plastic Comes From

Sankey · 10 → 3

Ten dominant source streams feed three vertical destinations — surface waters, water column, and seabed sediments. Single-use packaging dominates input volume, while abandoned fishing gear contributes disproportionately to long-lived, large-format debris.
