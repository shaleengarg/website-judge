Reading interpretation

Stable, but slipping.

A score of 62 places the global ocean in the stable band — above the stressed threshold but below the long-term mean of 67 last recorded in 2014. The pull is downward: four of ten goals lost ground year-over-year.

Best Goal81TOURISM & RECREATION
Worst Goal43BIODIVERSITY

Higher is healthier. The composite weights ten policy-relevant goals defined by Halpern et al. (2012) and updated annually by NOAA's Office of National Marine Sanctuaries.

SOURCE · NOAA OHI 2024 · n=3,924 STATIONS

−4 Net points lost since 2014. The composite ocean health score has slipped from 66 to 62 across the last decade, a pace of −0.4 points per year. At current trajectory, the index reaches the "stressed" band by 2049.

§ 05 / Benthic Snapshot

What the dial does not show.

What the index undercounts

  • Sub-200 m benthic ecosystems
  • Cumulative chemical stressors (PFAS, PCBs)
  • Underwater noise pollution
  • Genetic-level biodiversity loss

The lens widens, slowly
