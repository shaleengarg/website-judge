§ 01 · Distribution

The Reef Belt

Robinson · 23.5° N ↔ 23.5° S

Tropical reef-building corals occupy a narrow thermal envelope — roughly 23–29 °C — confining living reefs to a band straddling the equator. The six annotated systems below represent the largest contiguous reef provinces remaining on Earth.

Fig. 01 · World Coral Reef Distribution, 2024

Reef polygons scaled by area · Mercator-equivalent flat projection
N = 112 systems
Reef polygons combine GCRMN's Status of Coral Reefs of the World (2020) with NOAA's Allen Coral Atlas geomorphic baselayer. Annotation indices match the reef-profile cards below.
Source · GCRMN 2020 · Allen Coral Atlas v2 · NOAA CRW
Headline figure

14% of global reef — the area lost to bleaching, disease and storms between 2009 and 2018 alone, equivalent to all the coral currently living on Australia's reefs.

§ 03 · Trajectory

Bleaching Years, 1985–2024

DHW · Degree-heating weeks

Fig. 02 · Global Mass Bleaching Events

Reefs experiencing >8 DHW per year
2024 = 4th global event
Each bar denotes the share of monitored reef stations recording >8 degree-heating weeks of thermal stress in that year. Coral can recover from short events; the compressed 2014–17 and 2023–24 windows leave no recovery interval.
Source · NOAA Coral Reef Watch v3.1 · Eakin et al. 2022
Reefs bleached · 2024 77% 4th confirmed global event
DHW threshold 8.0 Beyond which mortality > 50%
Recovery window 10 yr Required between mass events
2.4×

Mass bleaching frequency since 2010 vs. the 1980–2000 baseline. The biological recovery interval has effectively collapsed: most reefs now bleach again before fast-growing branching corals can re-colonise.
