Method · 04

A six-phase engagement, calibrated for institutions.

Every engagement at Oblique Flux moves through the same six phases — Discovery through Debrief — but their depth varies by context. Below is the durable structure we use to scope, contract, and operate research-led brand and product programs for museums, labs, universities, and public-sector clients.

  1. Discovery
  2. Define
  3. Design
  4. Develop
  5. Deploy
  6. Debrief
Engagement note

Phases run sequentially but overlap by roughly 15%. A full program typically spans 18–26 weeks; standalone audits can compress to 4.

PHASE 01

Discovery

3–5 Weeks
Research-led Stakeholder mapping Field immersion

We begin by listening. The Discovery phase reconstructs the institution's recent decision history, audience mix, and operational constraints so subsequent strategy is grounded in evidence, not assumption.

SI Stakeholder Interviews 12–20 conversations across leadership, curatorial, and operations.
AA Audience Analysis Quant segmentation cross-referenced with on-site behaviour.
CL Competitive Landscape Peer institutions, adjacent sectors, comparable digital products.
HA Heritage Audit Archive, identity history, and prior strategic work review.
Outputs
  • Discovery report (40–60 pp.)
  • Stakeholder map & quotes book
  • Audience segment briefs
  • Annotated peer landscape
Client Responsibility

Provide access to internal stakeholders for interviews (approx. 1 hour each), share prior research and brand artifacts, and nominate a single point-of-contact for scheduling. Plan for ~10 hours of total client time across the phase.

PHASE 02

Define

2–3 Weeks
Strategy Positioning Workshop

We translate Discovery findings into a sharp strategic frame — the positioning, principles, and decision criteria that govern every downstream choice. Defined together, defended in writing.

PS Positioning Statement A single paragraph that survives the next ten years of choices.
BP Brand Principles 5–7 principles, each with a do/don't pair for designers.
NA Narrative Arc Long-form story that powers admissions, fundraising, and PR.
SC Success Criteria Measurable signals tied to mission and operations.
Outputs
  • Strategy brief
  • Positioning & principles deck
  • Messaging architecture
  • Success-criteria matrix
Client Responsibility

Attend a two-day in-person definition workshop (or two half-day remote sessions) with 6–10 decision-makers. Designate a strategy approver empowered to ratify the positioning by the close of week three.

PHASE 03

Design

5–8 Weeks
Identity Editorial Wayfinding UI

The Design phase resolves the strategic frame into visible, usable form: identity systems, editorial models, environmental graphics, and digital interfaces — each developed against the principles set in Define.

ID Identity System Mark, type, color, motion logic, and tone-of-voice rules.
CD Component Design Atomic UI library spec'd for the engineering handoff.
EL Editorial Layouts Print and web templates anchored to a shared typographic scale.
PR Prototype Reviews Three formal review gates with documented decisions.
Outputs
  • Identity guidelines (v1)
  • UI component library
  • Template set & specimens
  • Interactive prototype
Client Responsibility

Commit to three scheduled review gates with consistent attendees. Consolidate feedback into a single channel within 5 business days of each gate. Avoid mid-phase rescoping — additions land in a clearly logged change list for Phase 04.

PHASE 04

Develop

4–10 Weeks
Build CMS QA

Designs harden into production assets and shipping code. We work in two-week iterations with continuous QA, accessibility checks, and integration into the institution's content and infrastructure environment.

FE Front-End Build Semantic, accessible markup paired to the component library.
CM CMS Modeling Editor-friendly content types matched to editorial workflows.
A11 Accessibility QA WCAG 2.2 AA pass with manual screen-reader verification.
PA Production Assets Print-ready files, environmental specs, motion masters.
Outputs
  • Staging build & release notes
  • CMS schema & editor docs
  • Accessibility audit report
  • Print & environmental files
Client Responsibility

Provide hosting credentials and technical liaison by week one. Supply final copy and image rights at least two weeks before launch. Designate a UAT lead to coordinate testers and triage tickets through the staging period.

PHASE 05

Deploy

1–2 Weeks
Launch Rollout Training

Launch is a coordinated event, not an upload. We sequence go-live with content, communications, and operational readiness so the institution's internal teams are equipped to run the new system from day one.

RP Rollout Plan Timed cutover sequence with rollback contingency.
TR Team Training Two sessions for editors, designers, and comms staff.
LC Launch Comms Internal memo kit and external press-ready materials.
MN Monitoring Two-week stabilization window with daily check-ins.
Outputs
  • Production launch
  • Training recordings & manuals
  • Launch communications kit
  • Stabilization log
Client Responsibility

Free the internal team for two days of training during launch week. Coordinate cross-departmental announcements through your communications office. Plan for a moratorium on non-critical content changes for the first 10 days post-launch.

PHASE 06

Debrief

2 Weeks
Measurement Retrospective Handover

We close every engagement with a structured retrospective: what shipped, what shifted, what we learned, and what the institution should watch for in the months ahead. Debrief converts a project into institutional memory.

PM Performance Metrics Baseline vs. post-launch readings against Phase 02 criteria.
RE Retrospective Joint review with the working group, written and signed.
RM Roadmap 12-month optimization plan with owners and budgets.
HO Handover Pack Source files, repositories, contacts, license registry.
Outputs
  • Post-launch report
  • Retrospective document
  • 12-month roadmap
  • Complete handover archive
Client Responsibility

Attend the closing retrospective (half day) and ratify the 12-month roadmap. Nominate an internal steward to own the handover archive. Optional: agree a quarterly check-in cadence for the year following launch.
