Funding Coastal Stewardship

Grant Programs

Brackwater Marsh Conservancy distributes targeted funding to local restoration teams, educators, and emerging coastal scientists working along the Gulf. Three active programs are open for application in the 2024–2025 cycle.

Active Grant Programs

Restoration

Tidal Habitat Seed Fund

Community-Led Restoration Projects
Up to $15,000
Per Project

Seed funding for small-scale, community-driven restoration of tidal habitats across the Gulf coast. Awards support living shorelines, marsh replanting, oyster reef pilots, and hydrology repair on parcels under 25 acres.

Eligibility Criteria
  • 501(c)(3) organization or fiscally sponsored community group
  • Project site within 50 miles of Gulf coast tidewater
  • Demonstrated landowner or land manager partnership
  • One-year minimum monitoring plan included in proposal
Application OpensJanuary 15, 2025
DeadlineMarch 31, 2025
Award NoticeMay 15, 2025
Term18 months
Past Recipient
Aransas Bay Living Shoreline $14,200 awarded in 2023 to install 410 linear feet of oyster-bag breakwater protecting Spartina marsh from boat wake erosion.
Education

Estuary Education Initiative

K–12 Curriculum Development
$5,000
Per Award

Supports teachers, school districts, and informal educators developing place-based estuarine curriculum aligned with Gulf coast ecology. Awards fund classroom materials, field trip logistics, and educator stipends.

Eligibility Criteria
  • Licensed K–12 educator or accredited education nonprofit
  • Curriculum must reach minimum 60 students per cycle
  • Alignment with state science standards required
  • Open-licensing of final materials to the Conservancy library
Application OpensAugust 1, 2024
DeadlineOctober 15, 2024
Award NoticeDecember 1, 2024
TermOne school year
Past Recipient
Salt Marsh Scholars Program $5,000 awarded in 2022 to Mathis ISD to develop a six-week middle school unit on brackish water chemistry and indicator species.
Research

Coastal Resilience Fellowship

Emerging Scientists & Practitioners
$25,000
Stipend + Research

A one-year fellowship for early-career researchers studying sea-level rise, salinity dynamics, and habitat migration in Gulf tidal systems. Includes a $20,000 stipend plus $5,000 in research expenses and field access.

Eligibility Criteria
  • Graduate student or postdoctoral researcher within five years of terminal degree
  • Research focus on Gulf of Mexico coastal resilience
  • Faculty or institutional sponsor required
  • Quarterly site presence at Conservancy field stations
Application OpensSeptember 1, 2024
DeadlineNovember 30, 2024
Award NoticeFebruary 1, 2025
Term12 months
Past Recipient
Dr. Marcus Halloran $25,000 awarded in 2021 for sediment accretion modeling across four black mangrove sites; results published in Estuaries & Coasts.

How Funding Decisions Are Made

Each program is reviewed by a rotating panel of three scientific advisors, two community partners, and one Conservancy board member. Panels score proposals against published rubrics covering ecological merit, community benefit, feasibility, and long-term stewardship plans.

All scoring rubrics and prior-year award lists are published in our annual report. The Conservancy does not fund overhead costs above 15% of total project budget, and discourages duplicate applications across programs within a single cycle.
