Frequently Asked Questions

Is my donation tax-deductible?

Yes. Brackwater Marsh Conservancy is a registered 501(c)(3). All donations are tax-deductible to the fullest extent permitted by U.S. law. A receipt is emailed within 48 hours.

Can I designate my gift to a specific project?

Absolutely. During checkout, choose from restoration, education, or the Coastal Resilience Fellowship. Restricted gifts of $1,000+ may be tracked with quarterly impact updates.

How do I sign up to volunteer?

Visit our Volunteer page, review the eight open program roles, and submit an interest form. A coordinator follows up within five business days to schedule onboarding and orientation.

Do volunteers need prior experience?

Most roles require no prior experience — just curiosity and reliability. Specialized roles like Bird Survey Technician and Water Quality Sampler include free training sessions each spring.

When are grant applications open?

The Seed Fund accepts rolling applications. The Education Initiative opens each January, and the Fellowship cycle opens each March. See the Grants page for specific deadlines and required materials.

Who is eligible to apply for grants?

Eligibility varies by program. Most grants fund Gulf Coast nonprofits, educators, tribes, and emerging scientists working within Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, or the Florida Panhandle.

Can my school or class visit the field office?

Yes — we host K-12 group tours on Tuesdays and Thursdays during the school year. Email Aisha Bellamy at least three weeks in advance to coordinate scheduling and curriculum alignment.

How do I report a wetland concern or fish kill?

For active emergencies, contact Texas Parks & Wildlife first. For ongoing observations or restoration questions, email field@brackwatermarsh.org with location, date, and any photos.
