Get Involved

Volunteer With the Conservancy

From mudflat surveys at sunrise to classroom visits and grant editing, our volunteers are the lungs of the marsh. Choose a role that fits your schedule, your skills, and your love of the Gulf Coast.

Eight Ways to Pitch In

Each program is led by a staff coordinator and pairs new volunteers with experienced mentors. Training is provided; no prior fieldwork experience is required for most roles.

Field · Year-Round

Marsh Monitor

Walk fixed transects through Spartina stands recording vegetation density, erosion markers, and salinity. Data feeds our long-term restoration tracking and informs partner agency reports.

Time6 hrs / month
SkillsObservation, basic GPS
Field · Seasonal

Shoreline Cleanup Lead

Coordinate teams of 8–20 volunteers on monthly beach and tidal-flat debris removal events. Leads handle safety briefings, waste logging, and end-of-day weigh-ins at the depot.

TimeOne Saturday / month
SkillsGroup leadership
Education · School Year

School Outreach Educator

Visit K–12 classrooms across Nueces and Aransas counties to deliver our 45-minute estuary curriculum. Includes hands-on salinity demos and live brackish-water specimen handling.

Time4–8 hrs / month
SkillsPublic speaking
Horticulture · Weekly

Native Plant Nursery Assistant

Help propagate black mangrove, smooth cordgrass, and Louisiana iris seedlings at our Aransas Pass nursery. Tasks include potting, watering, label maintenance, and pre-planting hardening.

Time3 hrs / week
SkillsGardening basics
Science · Monthly

Water Quality Sampler

Collect monthly water samples at 14 fixed estuary stations, log temperature, dissolved oxygen, and turbidity, then deliver samples to the TAMU-CC partner lab for analysis.

Time5 hrs / month
SkillsCareful data entry
Science · Spring & Fall

Bird Survey Technician

Conduct point counts and shorebird census walks during spring and fall migrations. Volunteers identify species by sight and call, contributing data to regional flyway monitoring programs.

Time8 hrs / month seasonal
SkillsBird ID, binoculars
Remote · Flexible

Grant Writing Support

Assist staff with proofreading, narrative drafting, and budget formatting for federal and foundation grant applications. Most work is remote with weekly check-ins via phone or email.

Time4 hrs / week
SkillsWriting, editing
Field · Quarterly

Trail Maintenance Crew

Repair boardwalks, clear invasive growth, and refresh interpretive signage along our six public access trails. Quarterly weekend workdays include tools, gloves, and lunch on site.

TimeOne weekend / quarter
SkillsLight carpentry helpful

How to Join

Whether you've got an hour a month or every Saturday free, the path in is the same. New volunteers typically complete onboarding within two to three weeks of first contact.

  1. Submit an Interest Form

    Tell us which roles appeal to you, your availability, and any relevant experience. The form takes about five minutes to complete.

  2. Meet a Program Coordinator

    We'll schedule a short phone or in-person conversation to discuss expectations, match you to a team, and answer any questions about the work.

  3. Attend Orientation & Training

    Complete a half-day general orientation plus any role-specific training (data protocols, field safety, classroom curriculum, or tool handling).

  4. Start Your First Shift

    You'll be paired with a mentor for your first two outings. After that, you're a full member of the crew — and the marsh.

Ready to Begin?

Reach out to our volunteer coordinator and we'll match you with a role that fits your schedule and skills. Most volunteers are out in the field within three weeks.
