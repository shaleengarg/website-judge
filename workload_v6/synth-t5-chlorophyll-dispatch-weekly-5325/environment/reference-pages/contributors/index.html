The Hands Behind the Pages

Chlorophyll Dispatch is produced by twenty-four field journalists, scientists, illustrators and editors working across six continents. What follows is the standing masthead for the current editorial year, with beats, biographies and contribution counts as of the most recent press date.

A magazine, like a forest, is held together by the people who notice the small things — the falling leaf, the missing bird, the headline nobody else thought to write. These twenty-four colleagues are the canopy and the understory of every issue we publish.
— Maren Aldrick, Editor-in-Chief Filed from Reykjavík · 14 March 2024

Senior Fellows

04 Members · Tier I

Long-form commissioning editors and contributing scientists who set the magazine's editorial direction and serve on the editorial board for terms of three to five years.

Dr. Ines Vasquez-Morell

Senior Fellow, Biogeochemistry

A peatland biogeochemist trained at Wageningen and Stanford, Vasquez-Morell directs our long-running carbon flux dossier and has reported from twenty-two boreal sites since joining the masthead. Her 2022 essay on rewetting protocols was anthologised in Best American Science Writing.

Akira Ondaatje

Senior Fellow, Land Politics

A former parliamentary correspondent for The Colombo Review, Ondaatje covers land-tenure disputes, extractive concessions and indigenous sovereignty across South and Southeast Asia. His 2021 investigation into Sundarbans aquaculture leases prompted three regulatory reviews.

Land Rights · Policy063 PIECES

Prof. Helena Sørenson

Senior Fellow, Marine Systems

Sørenson holds the chair in Coastal Ecology at the University of Bergen and contributes our quarterly fjord dispatch. A veteran of fourteen North Atlantic research cruises, she writes with the unhurried clarity of someone who has spent her life waiting for tides.

Oceans · Fjords · Kelp029 PIECES

Tomás Berhane-Lago

Senior Fellow, Climate Justice

Berhane-Lago reports on the intersection of climate adaptation and human displacement, with extended assignments in the Sahel and the Horn of Africa. He co-edits our annual atlas of climate-vulnerable communities and lectures at the University of Addis Ababa.

Staff Writers

06 Members · Tier II

Full-time editorial staff producing the core reporting and analysis across each quarterly issue, with rotating coverage of legislative beats and ecological assignments.

Marisol Kierney

Senior Staff Writer

A graduate of the Missouri School of Journalism, Kierney leads our agricultural-runoff investigations and authored this issue's deep dive on nitrogen cascades. Before joining the Dispatch in 2019, she covered municipal water systems for three regional dailies in the upper Midwest.

Rivers · Agriculture078 PIECES

Devan Okafor

Staff Writer, Forests

Okafor covers tropical forest policy and the global timber trade, splitting his reporting between Yaoundé and Brussels. His four-part series on Congo Basin certification schemes earned the 2023 Goldsmith Prize for environmental reporting and a citation from the European Forest Institute.

Forests · Trade042 PIECES

Rosa Lindqvist

Staff Writer, Pollinators

Lindqvist's beat encompasses insect decline, agricultural pesticides and the small-scale apiarists carrying on against them. A trained entomologist, she joined the magazine after eight years at the Swedish Museum of Natural History and reports primarily from northern Europe and the Baltic.

Insects · Bees036 PIECES

Caleb Rusch

Staff Writer, Energy

Rusch reports on grid transitions, lithium extraction and the contested geography of renewable build-out. He has filed from the salars of Bolivia, the wind corridors of Oaxaca and the rare-earth processing belt of Inner Mongolia, often on assignments lasting many weeks.

Energy · Minerals055 PIECES

Yusra Khattab

Staff Writer, Water

Based in Amman, Khattab covers transboundary water politics across the Levant, the Nile basin and the Tigris–Euphrates system. Her reporting blends hydrological detail with patient diplomatic shoe-leather, and her dispatches frequently set the agenda for our spring issues.

Water · Borders044 PIECES

Jonas Trembath

Staff Writer, Cities

Trembath writes about urban ecology, green-space politics and the strange botany of derelict industrial sites. A former planner, he is the magazine's most prolific essayist on what he calls the “fourth-nature” landscapes — feral, accidental and quietly thriving — found at the edges of large cities.

Field Correspondents

10 Members · Tier III

Regional correspondents on rolling contract, filing dispatches from their resident bioregions and serving as the magazine's primary reporting network across six continents.

Pilar Echevarría

Correspondent, Patagonia

Echevarría files from the Aysén region, covering wool-economy collapse, glacial retreat and the long argument over hydropower in the Chilean fjords. A native of Coyhaique, she walks transects in winter that most reporters would not attempt in summer.

Ngozi Adeyemi

Correspondent, West Africa

Adeyemi reports from Lagos on coastal erosion, mangrove restoration and the politics of oil-spill remediation in the Niger Delta. Her reporting on the Ogoniland clean-up has been cited in two parliamentary inquiries and a federal-court remediation order.

Coasts · Oil023 PIECES

Henrik Aalto

Correspondent, Cairngorms

Aalto covers rewilding, deer-stalking economies and the slow return of the Caledonian pine forest. Based in Aviemore, he is the magazine's principal Highlands voice and contributes a recurring winter column on the language and folklore of Scots ecology.

Sundara Pillai

Correspondent, Sundarbans

Pillai files from the Bangladesh–India border deltas, where she has spent a decade documenting tiger-conflict zones, salinity creep and the women-led oyster cooperatives reshaping the local economy. She writes in Bengali and English and translates much of her own work.

How the issue is made

Chlorophyll Dispatch is set in Georgia for display, system-ui for body text and a monospaced face for marginalia. Each issue is edited from offices in Reykjavík, Lisbon and Montréal, printed on FSC-certified uncoated stock, and bound in signatures of sixteen pages. Editorial enquiries and pitches may be addressed to the relevant tier-lead at the email on the masthead page of the print edition.

Submissions are read on a rolling basis. Field correspondents are commissioned annually each January; staff positions open as they arise. The magazine accepts no advertising and is supported entirely by reader subscriptions and the Aldrick-Lago Foundation for Independent Environmental Journalism.
