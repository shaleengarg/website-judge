Three Failures, Three Geographies

Field reporting was conducted between March 2022 and August 2023. Each subject corridor was selected for divergent failure modes — structural, operational, and managerial — that together describe the texture of decline across Class II inland freight.
CASE 01 · STRUCTURAL
01
The Wabash Crossing
Lafayette, Indiana Mile 412.6 · Wabash Sub. CRITICAL

The Wabash truss bridge has carried freight since 1923. A century of frost-heave, river ice, and a 311% rise in axle loading has produced what FRA inspectors classified in February 2023 as a "Priority Yellow" condition — one designation below mandatory closure.

Riveted plate connections at the south abutment show measurable shear migration. Engineers from the Indiana DOT recorded a 7.2mm horizontal drift between 2018 and 2023, accelerating in the last 14 months. Two of the bridge's eight stringers were stamped non-compliant in the most recent walk-through, yet daily train counts have not decreased: 31 freights per 24-hour cycle continue to pass over the span.

Owner-operator Norfolk Southern budgeted $4.2M for partial deck replacement in 2021. As of this report, $1.1M has been expended — primarily on inspection scaffolding. A 2024 closure window has been "deferred pending corridor-level review."

CASE 02 · OPERATIONAL
02
The Flathead Subdivision
Whitefish to Libby, Montana 147 route-miles MONITORED

Across the Flathead Subdivision, the failure is not a single bridge but a 147-mile averaging-out of small disrepairs. Section crews have been reduced by 41% since 2009 consolidation, and the territory's three remaining maintenance-of-way foremen each oversee a beat that, in 1990, would have employed eleven men. Track geometry cars recorded 287 Class 2 exceptions in the last twelve-month window — most cited gauge widening through curve-heavy segments near Marias Pass.

The line carries grain east and intermodal containers west. When a 2022 derailment near Essex spilled 14 cars of soy meal into the Middle Fork, federal investigators traced root cause to a sun-kinked rail that had been flagged — and dispatched — three weeks earlier.

“We don't fix the railroad anymore. We babysit it until somebody else's shift.” — Section foreman, 22 years on the Flathead Sub.

BNSF's 2023 capital plan allocates $89M to the Northern Transcon, of which a disclosed $6.4M is earmarked for Flathead-specific tie renewal. Industry analysts estimate the deferred-maintenance backlog on this subdivision alone at $112M — a figure the carrier has not publicly contested.

CASE 03 · MANAGERIAL
03
The Gulf Coast Connector
Lake Charles, Louisiana Junction Mile 0–18 DEFERRED REPAIR
A short-line junction serving four petrochemical terminals, the Gulf Coast Connector has been the subject of seven discrete inspection events since 2019. Each surfaced overlapping defects; none triggered remediation. The timeline below reconstructs the regulatory record from FRA, state DOT, and operator filings obtained via FOIA.
04 · 2019 · FRA
Initial Tie Defect Report
First federal inspection flags 412 defective ties between MP 4.2 and MP 11.8. Operator issued 90-day cure notice. No public response filed.
ENTRY · 01
11 · 2020 · STATE
Bridge 7 Load Restriction
Louisiana DOTD lowers permitted axle loading on the Calcasieu River crossing from 286,000 lb to 263,000 lb pending fatigue review.
ENTRY · 02
06 · 2021 · OPERATOR
Capital Plan Withdrawn
Short-line owner withdraws $11.4M rehabilitation submission citing "reassessment of through-traffic forecasts." No replacement plan filed for 22 months.
ENTRY · 03
02 · 2022 · FRA
Second Cure Notice
Repeat inspection finds 380 of the original 412 tie defects unaddressed; 117 new defects logged. Penalty referred to civil division.
ENTRY · 04
09 · 2022 · INCIDENT
Minor Derailment, MP 8.4
Two tank cars carrying liquid sulfur leave the track at 12 mph. No release. Cause: gauge spread on Class 1 trackage that should have been removed from service.

07 · 2023 · FRA
Operating Restriction Issued
Maximum authorized speed reduced to 10 mph over 14 miles. Operator appeals. As of filing, restriction is active and appeal is unscheduled.
