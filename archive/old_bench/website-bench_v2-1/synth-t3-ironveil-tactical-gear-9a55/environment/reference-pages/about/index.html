About Ironveil — Est. 2014

Built for Operators. Trusted by Civilians.

Ironveil was founded in 2014 in a one-room workshop on the south side of Boise, Idaho, by two friends who couldn't find a plate carrier that met their standards at a price civilians could justify. What began as a side project — cutting Cordura panels on a folding table after work — has grown into a vertically integrated operation supplying preparedness enthusiasts, range instructors, and security professionals across all fifty states.

Every Ironveil load-bearing system is built from US-sourced 500D Cordura, milled in Quakertown, Pennsylvania, and bonded with mil-spec Berry-compliant thread. We don't subcontract overseas, we don't white-label other manufacturers, and we don't ship a product line we haven't personally beaten on in the field. If a stitch fails, we replace the garment. That is not a marketing promise — it is the only standard we know how to operate by.

Our sewing is done in partnership with three veteran-owned workshops in Idaho and eastern Oregon, employing former Army and Marine textile specialists. This partnership keeps our lead times short, our quality consistent, and our supply chain accountable. When you buy an Ironveil carrier, you are paying the people who served, who train, and who carry what they build.

Marcus Yellen

Founder & CEO

Former 11B and range safety officer, Marcus founded Ironveil after a decade of cycling through carriers that didn't survive a single deployment rotation. He oversees product engineering and material sourcing from the Boise workshop.

PC

Priya Caldwell

Head of Design

Priya leads pattern engineering and modular system design. Trained in industrial textile design at RISD, she joined Ironveil in 2017 and authored the MK-series cummerbund geometry now standard across the product line.
