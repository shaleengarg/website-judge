The Method

Cooking the Short Rib

Six steps from cold cast iron to glossy, glaze-lacquered ribs.

  1. Sear the ribs. Pat the bone-in short ribs dry and season generously with salt. Heat a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high until it just begins to smoke. Sear the ribs on all four sides until deeply browned, about 3 minutes per side. Transfer to a plate.
  2. Build the braise base. Pour off most of the fat. Add the sliced shallots and crushed garlic and cook until softened, 4 minutes. Stir in the tomato paste and smoked paprika and toast for another minute until the mixture darkens and turns fragrant.
  3. Add stock and aromatics. Return the ribs to the pot bone-side up. Pour in the beef stock until it reaches roughly two-thirds up the sides of the meat. Tuck in the thyme sprigs and bring everything to a bare simmer.
  4. Oven-braise at 325°F. Cover with a tight-fitting lid and slide into a 325°F oven. Braise undisturbed for 3 hours, or until a fork slips into the meat with no resistance. Carefully lift the ribs out and strain the braising liquid.
  5. Reduce the rye glaze. In a small saucepan, combine the rye whiskey, brown sugar, whole-grain mustard, apple cider vinegar, and a cup of the strained braising liquid. Simmer over medium heat for 10–12 minutes, until the glaze coats the back of a spoon.
  6. Finish and serve. Brush the warm ribs generously with the rye glaze, then return them to the oven uncovered for 5 minutes to set the lacquer. Spoon over any remaining glaze and serve immediately with creamy polenta or buttered rye toast.
