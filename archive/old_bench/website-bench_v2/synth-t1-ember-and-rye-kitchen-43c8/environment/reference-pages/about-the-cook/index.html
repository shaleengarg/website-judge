About the Cook

Maren Solís

Cook, recipe developer, low-and-slow believer.

Portrait — Maren in the kitchen

Maren Solís grew up in Albuquerque, where the smell of green chile roasting in the fall did most of her early culinary teaching. After a decade on the line at a handful of restaurants — from a barbecue joint outside Santa Fe to a small bistro in Brooklyn — she traded the rush of service for a quieter kitchen and now develops recipes focused on slow-cooked, smoke-forward comfort food.

On Braising

Her philosophy is simple, and she repeats it often:

"Braising is patience with a payoff. You don't chase tenderness — you wait for it, and you season it on the way in."

She believes the best braises are built in layers: a hard sear for color, a deep fond, a sturdy aromatic base, and just enough liquid to keep things honest. Everything else is time.

About This Recipe

This short rib recipe took eleven test batches to finalize. Earlier versions were sweeter, or boozier, or simply too polite. The current glaze — rye whiskey reduced with brown sugar, whole-grain mustard, and a clean hit of cider vinegar — is the one Maren kept coming back to long after the notebooks were full.

She kept the spice list short on purpose. Smoked paprika does the heavy lifting; the rye finishes the sentence.
