The Cold Descent
of Ocean Temperature

From sun-warmed surface waters to the near-freezing dark of the hadal zone, ocean temperature drops sharply across a narrow band called the thermocline. Below 1,000 meters the sea is uniformly cold — a global cold reservoir that drives the planet's climate machinery.

Thermocline

A sharp transition layer between 200–1000m where temperature plunges from ~20°C to under 5°C. Acts as a thermal barrier preventing mixing of warm surface and cold deep waters.

POINTS TO 200–1000m

Thermohaline Loop

Density-driven circulation: cold, salty water sinks at the poles and flows along the seafloor for ~1,000 years before upwelling, redistributing heat across every ocean basin.

POINTS TO ABYSSAL · 4000m

Polar Meltwater

Fresh, cold runoff from melting ice sheets lowers surface salinity at high latitudes — weakening the sinking that powers global circulation and threatening to slow the entire conveyor.
