Volunteer with Hollow Creek

Every restored acre begins with someone pulling on boots before dawn. Volunteers are the steady hands behind our work.

Our volunteers contribute roughly 4,800 hours annually across seasonal events from March through November — sowing prairie seed, monitoring stream chemistry, mentoring schoolyard naturalists, and clearing miles of trail along the Ozark plateau. Whether you can give a single Saturday or a standing weekly commitment, there is a crew that fits your schedule and skills.

Recurring Volunteer Roles

⛰

Trail Clearing Crew

Maintain access paths through restoration sites using hand tools and light brush gear. Crews work in small teams led by an experienced steward.

2nd Saturday, Mar–Nov
❀

Seed Harvest Team

Collect, clean, and catalog seed from prairie remnants for the Ozark Prairie Seed Library. Training provided; gentle pace, lots of bending.

Weekly, Aug–Oct
≈

Water Quality Monitors

Sample tributaries of Finley Creek for turbidity, nitrates, and macroinvertebrates. Data feeds our community science dashboard.

Monthly, year-round
✕

Invasive Species Patrol

Identify and remove bush honeysuckle, sericea lespedeza, and callery pear. Physical work; gloves and loppers supplied at each event.

1st Sunday, Apr–Nov
✎

School Program Mentors

Co-lead outdoor learning sessions at partner schools through the Schoolyard Habitat Grants program. Background check required.

2–4 hrs / month, school year
⚘

Native Plant Nursery Helpers

Transplant seedlings, water trays, and prep plugs for spring distribution at our greenhouse outside Eureka Springs. Indoor-friendly role.

Wednesdays, Feb–May
