About

I'm Mara Veltri. I write ad copy for a living in Chicago — short, persuasive sentences for products I mostly don't use — and I haven't slept properly since somewhere around 2017. My doctor calls it chronic onset insomnia. I call it the long shift. Most nights I'm awake from about 1am until the light comes in through the kitchen blinds, which is a lot of hours to fill if you don't have a hobby.

Ink & Insomnia started in October 2021 at a diner on North Lincoln Avenue, the kind with a laminated menu and a coffee pot that never quite empties. I'd been sitting in the same vinyl booth for about three hours, scribbling in a paperback notebook, when I realized the things I was writing weren't ads. They were essays. Honest ones, the kind I'd never get away with at work. I typed them up the next morning and bought a domain before I lost my nerve.

What I hope you take from any of this: that being awake at strange hours is not a personal failing, that secondhand bookstores still exist for a reason, and that an empty diner at 3am is one of the last public places in America where nobody expects anything from you. If even one of these essays keeps you company on a bad night, then the insomnia has finally paid for itself.

All opinions are my own. All typos are the insomnia's fault.
