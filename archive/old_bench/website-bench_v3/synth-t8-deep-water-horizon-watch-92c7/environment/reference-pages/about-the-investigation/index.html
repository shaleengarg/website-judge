Methodology & Ethics Statement

Five years, eleven nations, one method: verify, verify, verify.

The Tethys Journalism Collective spent sixty-three months reconstructing the movements, ownership, and catch records of 847 vessels operating in or transiting the South Coral Sea between January 2019 and December 2024. This page documents how that work was done, by whom, with what money, and against which standards we asked to be held.

Our core dataset began with AIS transponder records cross-referenced against four national vessel registries. Where transponders went dark — a recurring pattern at the boundary of contested EEZs — we triangulated using Sentinel-2 optical imagery and Sentinel-1 synthetic aperture radar, applying a 96-hour interpolation window. Two independent geospatial analysts reviewed every flagged track before it entered our published incident map.

"If a finding could not be corroborated through two unrelated source streams, it did not appear in this report. Silence is preferable to error."

Field correspondents conducted 142 on-the-record interviews across Papua New Guinea, the Solomon Islands, Queensland, the Philippines, Vanuatu, and the Federated States of Micronesia. Sources were offered confidentiality only where verifiable retaliation risk existed; in those cases, a senior editor reviewed the threat assessment and the source's identity is held in encrypted offline storage accessible to two named individuals.

We declined to publish three significant findings during the reporting window because corroborating evidence remained single-sourced. Two of those threads were later confirmed and appear in the Field Dispatches chapter. The third remains under investigation and will be released only if the standard is met.

Statistical claims are reproducible. Every figure in the Data Atlas links to a notebook in our public methodology repository, including the exact AIS query, vessel filter, and statistical model used. Reviewers from the University of Tasmania's IMAS and the Stimson Center's Environmental Security Program audited our methodology in May 2024; both reviews are published in full alongside this report.

We accepted no funding from any party with commercial fishing interests, any government with jurisdictional standing in the South Coral Sea, or any party named in our investigation. Tethys correspondents are bound by the IFJ Global Charter of Ethics and the collective's internal conflict-of-interest registry, updated quarterly.

This investigation will remain open. Tip line and secure-drop instructions are available in the report's footer. Corrections will be timestamped and preserved.

Editorial Independence Statement

No funder, source, advertiser, or institutional partner reviewed this report prior to publication. Editorial decisions — including what to publish, what to withhold, and how to frame findings — were made solely by the named correspondents and the managing editor. The Tethys Journalism Collective is a worker-owned, non-profit press cooperative registered in Oslo, Norway, and operates under bylaws that prohibit any external party from exercising editorial influence.
