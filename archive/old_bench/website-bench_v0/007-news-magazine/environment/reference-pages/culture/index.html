The Quiet Renaissance of Regional Opera Houses

From a converted granary in Saskatchewan to a refurbished theater in Santa Fe, a generation of company directors is rethinking what opera can be — and who it can be for. The productions are smaller, the repertoire more daring, and the audiences, unexpectedly, are growing younger.

Our critic spent three months traveling the new circuit, attending nineteen performances and speaking with the singers, conductors, and donors who have made the revival possible. The result is less a survey than a portrait of a fragile, improbable flourishing.

By Vivienne Marlowe · Critic-at-Large
