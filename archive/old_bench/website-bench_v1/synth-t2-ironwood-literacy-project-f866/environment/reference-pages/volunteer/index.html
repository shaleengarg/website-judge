Volunteer With Us

Our volunteers are the heart of every reading circle across the hollers and hill towns we serve. Most roles ask for 3–5 hours per week, with flexible scheduling around your life. New volunteers complete a two-evening training (held quarterly in Boone, Hazard, and online) covering adult learning principles, dignity-first tutoring, and our curriculum. No teaching background is required — just patience, warmth, and a steady commitment to your learner.

Volunteer Roles

  1. One-on-One Reading Tutor

    Meet weekly with a matched adult learner to work through phonics, fluency, and reading goals they set themselves.

  2. Small Group Facilitator

    Lead a 4–6 person reading circle at a host library, church, or community center using our guided lesson packets.

  3. Writing & Letters Mentor

    Support learners building practical writing skills — filling forms, writing letters, navigating job applications.

  4. Driver & Transportation Helper

    Provide reliable rides to and from sessions for learners in counties where public transit is limited or unavailable.

  5. Materials & Library Steward

    Organize, repair, and rotate our donated book collections across the eight regional resource closets we operate.

  6. Event & Outreach Volunteer

    Staff a table at county fairs, health clinics, and church socials to help neighbors learn about our free programs.

Get Started

Tell us a little about you — a coordinator will reach out within five business days.

We follow up by phone or email. Training dates are shared after your application is reviewed.

About Ironwood

The Ironwood Literacy Project provides free adult reading programs across rural Appalachia, partnering with libraries, churches, and community centers to meet learners where they live.
