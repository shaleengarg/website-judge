Brussels Pushes a Quiet Reset on Eastern Trade Corridors

After months of stalled negotiations, European Commission staff have begun circulating a revised draft that would unlock cross-border rail subsidies and ease tariffs on grain and rare-earth shipments. Diplomats say the language is deliberately narrow — a tactical retreat designed to keep three holdout capitals at the table without inviting domestic backlash.

Kenyan Court Ruling Reshapes the Continent's Data Privacy Map

A landmark decision in Nairobi last week extended consumer protections to cross-border processing for the first time, and the ripple is already moving south. Regulators in Pretoria and Lagos have signaled they will follow, while telecoms warn that compliance costs could reach a billion dollars over five years.

The promise was simple: turn the world's largest nickel reserves into the spine of a clean-energy economy. Two years in, the smelters around Sulawesi run mostly on coal, the rivers run an unfamiliar orange, and the buyers in Detroit and Munich are quietly drafting backup procurement plans.

Six months after the shock devaluation, headline inflation is finally cooling, but the central bank's reserves remain dangerously thin and street protests have returned to Plaza de Mayo. Inside the Casa Rosada, advisors are debating whether to pivot toward orthodox tightening or double down on chainsaw cuts.
