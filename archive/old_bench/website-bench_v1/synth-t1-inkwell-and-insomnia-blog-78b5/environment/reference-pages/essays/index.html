Against the Tidy Ending

A long argument with the contemporary short story, which I love, and with its habit of resolving every ambiguity into a small, photographable epiphany before the last paragraph.

An essay in seven uneven sections, written across as many sleepless nights, asking whether the shape of a piece of writing should follow the shape of the hours that produced it.
