About

My name is Vera Osei. I write essays, journal entries, and slow reading notes — most of them composed at hours when the rest of the house has gone quiet. I am not a journalist or a critic by training; I am a reader who eventually got tired of keeping her marginalia to herself. This blog is the long version of those margins.

I started writing at night by accident and kept doing it on purpose. The first essays here were drafted at 1 a.m. on weeknights after stacks of student papers had been marked, because that was the only hour the day stopped making demands. I came to love the particular attention that arrives after midnight — slower, more forgiving of digression, less interested in being clever. Most of what I publish here is still written in that window.

This is not a productivity blog, a self-improvement project, or a newsletter about the writing life. I will not tell you what to read next, only what I read and what it did to me. There are no affiliate links, no courses, no five-step frameworks. If a post seems to be arguing toward a tidy conclusion, please assume I lost my nerve at the end and ought to be argued with.

I live in Accra, Ghana. By day I teach literature at a secondary school, which is to say I spend most of my waking hours trying to convince teenagers that a sentence can be a place you stand inside.
