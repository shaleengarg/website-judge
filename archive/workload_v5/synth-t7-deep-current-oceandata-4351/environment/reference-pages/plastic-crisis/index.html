The Plastic Crisis

8 million tons enter the ocean every year — tracing the river-to-seafloor lifecycle of marine plastic.
Synthesis 2024

From rivers and rigging to the abyssal plain.

Roughly 380 million tons of plastic is produced each year. Of that, an estimated 8 Mt escapes containment and reaches the ocean — 80% via 1,000 high-discharge rivers, 18% from coastal dumping and tourism, and 2% from fisheries, shipping, and aquaculture gear. Once afloat, currents sort the debris into five subtropical gyres before fragmentation and biofouling drag it down to the seafloor, where the deepest microplastic was recovered at 10,975 m in the Mariana Trench.

Sources UNEP 2023 Jambeck et al. 2021 OECD Global Plastics Outlook NOAA Marine Debris The Ocean Cleanup
⚠ Trajectory

Without intervention, plastic flux into the ocean is projected to triple by 2040, reaching ~29 Mt/yr. The accumulated stock could exceed 600 Mt — outweighing the biomass of all wild marine vertebrates combined.

The Five Gyres

Subtropical accumulation zones, ranked by debris density.

N. Pacific 1.6M km² · 79,000 t The Great Pacific Garbage Patch — twice the size of Texas; 1.8 trillion pieces.
N. Atlantic 1.1M km² · 31,000 t Sargasso convergence; 200,000 pieces/km² near Bermuda.
Indian Ocean 0.9M km² · 29,000 t Anchored between Madagascar and Australia; under-surveyed.
S. Pacific 2.6M km² · 22,000 t Easter Island sector; lower density but vast extent.
S. Atlantic 0.8M km² · 18,000 t Smallest gyre, dominated by Brazil & W. Africa coastal sources.
