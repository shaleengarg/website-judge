Regional Warming Signatures

— Three ocean basins · 1900–2024

Warming is not uniform. The Arctic Ocean has warmed nearly four times faster than the global mean, while the Southern Ocean's deep mixing has temporarily buffered surface heat. The Tropical Pacific oscillates with ENSO but trends upward decade over decade.

Tipping point advisory · AMOC

The Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation has weakened ~15% since the mid-20th century. Sustained surface warming combined with Greenland meltwater could push the AMOC past a collapse threshold within this century, with cascading consequences for European climate, monsoon stability, and tropical fisheries.
