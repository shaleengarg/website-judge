Vital Signs · Nine Indicators

Ocean Vital Signs Dashboard

Nine peer-reviewed indicators describe the ocean's biological, chemical and physical state. Teal markers denote stable or recovering systems; coral markers signal trajectories that exceed planetary boundary thresholds. All metrics are cross-validated against IPCC AR6 working-group estimates.

Healthy / stable Crisis / declining 1850 baseline
Surface pH
7.9pH

Down from 8.1 in 1850 — a 30% rise in hydrogen-ion concentration. Calcifying organisms face significant shell-formation stress.

Δ −0.20 since 1850 · IPCC AR6
Dissolved Oxygen
−2.0%

Open-ocean oxygen has dropped 2% since 1960, with hypoxic dead zones expanding fourfold. Mid-water species face habitat compression.

700+ dead zones · Global Ocean Oxygen Network
Coral Coverage Lost
50%

Half the world's coral reefs have died since 1950. Mass bleaching now occurs roughly every six years versus once per generation.

1950–2024 · GCRMN status report
Sea Level Rise
3.3mm/yr

Global mean sea level is rising 2.5× faster than the 20th-century average. Roughly one billion people live within 10 m of high tide.

Satellite altimetry · NASA JPL 1993–2023
Arctic Sea-Ice (Sept)
4.2M km²

September minimum extent has shrunk 40% since 1979. The Arctic could see ice-free summers as soon as the 2030s under current trajectories.

NSIDC Sea Ice Index · 1979–2023
Fish Biomass Index
−38%

Large predatory fish stocks have collapsed 90% since pre-industrial baselines. 34% of assessed fisheries are now harvested unsustainably.

FAO SOFIA · Sea Around Us 2022
Phytoplankton Density
−40%

Global phytoplankton concentrations have declined ~40% since 1950, weakening the base of the marine food web and ocean carbon sink.

Boyce et al. · Nature 2010 cont'd
Microplastic Conc.
170T particles

An estimated 170 trillion plastic particles now float in the ocean surface — a 10× increase since 2005. Found in every depth zone sampled.

5 Gyres Institute · 2023 census
Ocean Heat Content
+345ZJ

The ocean has absorbed 345 zettajoules of excess energy since 1958 — equivalent to detonating five Hiroshima bombs every second for 65 years.

NOAA NCEI · 0–2000m anomaly

Critical
Six of nine indicators outside safe operating limits

Acidification, deoxygenation, coral loss, sea-level rise, ice extent, biomass, plankton, microplastics and heat content all exceed pre-industrial ranges. Two — heat content and acidification — are accelerating.

The ocean is not an environment that surrounds us — it is the operating system of the biosphere, and right now we are overwriting its code. — Dr. Lena Marquez · Director, Deep Current Observatory
