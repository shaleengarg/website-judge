§ 5.2 — Getting Here

Public Transport & Approach

The gallery is most easily reached by metro line D or E to Rijnhaven, followed by a nine-minute walk south across the Rijnhavenbrug. Tram 25 from Rotterdam Centraal stops at Katendrechtse Hoofd; from there it is six minutes on foot along Veerlaan. Bus 76 connects directly to the warehouse district every twenty minutes on weekday afternoons. A waterbus from Erasmusbrug docks at the Katendrecht pier on Saturdays only.

Cyclists may use the free racks beside the loading bay on the eastern wall of the building; the gallery does not operate dedicated car parking, but municipal lots on Brede Hilledijk are a four-minute walk away. We strongly recommend arriving on foot — the approach along the harbor edge is, in our view, part of the work.

§ 5.3 — Accessibility

For Every Visitor

Obsidian Fold occupies a single ground-floor exhibition hall with a level concrete surface throughout, accessible by a 1.4m-wide ramp at the north entrance. The threshold is flush; door widths exceed 90cm; an accessible WC with grab-rails is located adjacent to the reading room. Seating is provided every 12 meters along the principal viewing wall, and large-print catalogues are available at the reception desk in Dutch and English.

Service animals are welcome. We can arrange BSL-interpreted or audio-described tours on request with two weeks' notice. The lighting in the hall is intentionally low (approximately 50 lux at the work surface, in line with conservation standards for paper-based media); visitors with low vision are encouraged to ask staff for a hand-held task lamp at the desk.

“The work asks to be seen slowly. We have arranged the light accordingly.” — Iris van der Velde, Director
