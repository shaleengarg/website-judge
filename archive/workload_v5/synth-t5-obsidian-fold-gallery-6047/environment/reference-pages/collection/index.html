The Collection

Twelve works · risograph & letterpress · editions of 12 to 60

Strata & Sediment gathers twelve large-format works produced in the gallery's basement press between September 2023 and February 2024. Each piece interrogates the slow accretion of mark on paper — the way a single sheet, run through the drum seven, ten, sometimes fourteen times, accumulates a history that no screen can flatten. The works are listed below in the order they appear in the south gallery; price reflects edition number and condition. NFS denotes works on loan from private collections.

On the Process

The Risograph Method

A risograph is a stencil duplicator developed in Japan in 1986: ink is forced through a wax-coated master onto paper at low pressure, one colour at a time. The drum holds soy-based ink in cartridges — burnt sienna, federal blue, fluorescent pink — and each layer must dry, often overnight, before the sheet returns to the machine for the next pass.

The process is famously imprecise. Registration drifts by a half-millimetre; ink density wavers; the rollers leave a faint, satisfying grit. We do not correct these things. They are the medium speaking back. Our editions typically run between 12 and 60, hand-trimmed on a 1962 Krause guillotine, and signed verso in soft graphite.
